# Behavioral & HR - 200+ Interview Q&A

### Q1: Tell me about yourself.
**Answer:** Structure: 1) Current role (Agentic AI intern at Krip AI, building FastAPI microservices). 2) Past experience (AI Agent dev intern, Data Science intern). 3) Projects (GuardrailZ, MigratorGen). 4) What I'm looking for (YC startup where I can build production AI systems). Keep to 60 seconds, relate to the role.

### Q2: Why do you want to work at our company?
**Answer:** Research the company first. Connect to: their tech stack, their mission/industry, specific products you like, where you can contribute based on your skills. Avoid generic answers about "growth" or "learning".

### Q3: Tell me about a challenging project.
**Answer:** Use STAR: Situation (GuardrailZ needed 50+ security guardrails), Task (detect+prevent prompt injection), Action (built modular NextJS app with regex + LLM validation, configurable profiles), Result (comprehensive security suite). Focus on YOUR contribution.

### Q4: How do you handle disagreement with a teammate?
**Answer:** Listen first, understand their perspective. Focus on data/evidence over ego. Propose experiments to test both approaches. Escalate only if necessary. Show you prioritize team outcome over being right.

### Q5: Tell me about a time you failed.
**Answer:** Choose real failure, show self-awareness. What you learned, how you improved. Example: "Underestimated time for a feature, missed deadline. Now I break tasks into smaller chunks and communicate early about blockers."

### Q6: Where do you see yourself in 5 years?
**Answer:** "In 5 years, I want to be a senior engineer who leads technical decisions, mentors juniors, and builds production systems that impact millions. I want to grow with the company."

### Q7: Why did you leave your previous role?
**Answer:** Be positive. "Looking for more impact, wanted to work on production systems at scale, wanted to work with modern stack." Never badmouth previous employer.

### Q8: What are your strengths?
**Answer:** Pick 3 relevant to the role. FastAPI/Python backend, AI/LLM integration, full-stack with NextJS. Back with specific examples from your projects.

### Q9: What are your weaknesses?
**Answer:** Honest weakness + improvement plan. "I sometimes go deep into optimization too early. I now set time limits and focus on working solution first, optimize after."

### Q10: How do you prioritize tasks?
**Answer:** Impact vs effort matrix. Critical path first. Communicate with team. Use tools (Jira, linear). Break large tasks into smaller chunks. Re-evaluate priorities daily.

### Q11: Explain a technical concept to a non-technical person.
**Answer:** Use analogies. Example: "RAG is like giving a student (LLM) a textbook to reference during an open-book test, instead of expecting them to know everything from memory."

### Q12: Describe your ideal work environment.
**Answer:** "Fast-paced, collaborative, high ownership. Clear goals with autonomy to figure out how. Strong engineering culture with code reviews and testing. Remote-friendly with async communication."

### Q13: How do you stay updated with technology?
**Answer:** Twitter/X (AI/ML accounts), GitHub trending, Hacker News, blog posts, building side projects (GuardrailZ, Workflow-Canvas), contributing to open source (Sim Studio, Agno).

### Q14: Tell me about your open source contributions.
**Answer:** Sim Studio: SSRF protection, localhost validation. Agno: Milvus reranking, JSON filter fix, proxy config for Crawl4AI. Explain the specific problems you solved.

### Q15: Why should we hire you?
**Answer:** "I have hands-on experience with exactly your stack: Python/FastAPI backend, AI/LLM integration, NextJS frontend. I'm shipping real products (GuardrailZ, Workflow-Canvas), contribute to open source, and learn fast. I'm excited to build production systems that matter."
