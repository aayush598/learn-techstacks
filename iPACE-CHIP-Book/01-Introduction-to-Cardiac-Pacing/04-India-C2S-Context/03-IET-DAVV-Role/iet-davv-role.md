# IET-DAVV Role in the iPACE-CHIP Project

## 1. Introduction

The **Institute of Engineering and Technology (IET), Devi Ahilya Vishwavidyalaya (DAVV)**, located in Indore, Madhya Pradesh, India, serves as a pivotal implementation partner for the Chips to Startups (C2S) program and the home institution for the iPACE-CHIP project. This chapter provides a comprehensive overview of IET-DAVV's role, its Electronic and Telecommunication Engineering Department, the research leadership, facilities, and collaborative opportunities that make it a suitable hub for indigenous pacemaker chip development.

## 7. iPACE-CHIP Development Plan at IET-DAVV

### 7.1 Technical Roadmap

| Phase | Timeline | Activity | Lead | Deliverable |
|-------|----------|----------|------|-------------|
| **Phase 1: Requirements** | 2024 Q1-Q2 | Clinical requirements, specifications, literature review | Dr. Neema | Requirements specification document |
| **Phase 2: Architecture** | 2024 Q2-Q3 | Chip architecture, block-level design, specifications | Dr. Neema + Dr. Sindal | Architecture specification |
| **Phase 3: Analog Design** | 2024 Q3-2025 Q1 | Sensing amplifiers, output drivers, PLL, references | Dr. Sindal | Analog design schematics + simulation |
| **Phase 4: Digital Design** | 2024 Q3-2025 Q1 | RTL design of timing, mode control, telemetry | Dr. Neema | Verified RTL code |
| **Phase 5: Verification** | 2025 Q1-Q2 | Functional verification, formal verification | Team | Verification report |
| **Phase 6: Physical Design** | 2025 Q2-Q3 | Place and route, timing closure, sign-off | Team | GDSII file |
| **Phase 7: Tape-out** | 2025 Q3-Q4 | MPW fabrication run | Team + foundry | Silicon samples |
| **Phase 8: Validation** | 2026 Q1-Q2 | Silicon testing, functional validation | Team | Test report |
| **Phase 9: Integration** | 2026 Q2-Q3 | System integration, lead testing, packaging | Team + partners | Working prototype |
| **Phase 10: Certification** | 2026 Q3-2027 | CDSCO/BIS certification preparation | Team + regulatory | Certification application |

### 7.2 iPACE-CHIP Design Specifications (Preliminary)

| Parameter | Specification | Design Owner |
|-----------|--------------|-------------|
| **Pacing modes** | VVI, VVIR (Phase 1); DDD, DDDR (Phase 2) | Dr. Neema |
| **Technology node** | 180nm CMOS (Phase 1); 65nm (Phase 2) | Team |
| **Supply voltage** | 1.8V (core), 3.3V (I/O), 5-10V (output) | Dr. Sindal |
| **Power consumption** | < 15 uA average | Dr. Sindal |
| **R-wave sensing** | 2-25 mV, programmable sensitivity | Dr. Sindal |
| **P wave sensing** | 0.2-4 mV, programmable sensitivity | Dr. Sindal |
| **Pacing output** | 0.25-7.5V programmable, 0.05-1.5 ms pulse width | Dr. Sindal |
| **Rate range** | 30-180 bpm programmable | Dr. Neema |
| **AV delay** | 60-350 ms programmable (DDD mode) | Dr. Neema |
| **Sensor interface** | Accelerometer (3-axis MEMS) | Dr. Neema |
| **Telemetry** | 402-405 MHz MICS band or BLE | Dr. Neema |
| **Memory** | 4KB+ EEPROM/FRAM for parameters | Dr. Neema |
| **Chip size** | < 4 mm x 4 mm (target) | Team |
| **Package** | Ceramic or hermetic titanium compatible | Team |

### 7.3 Subsystem Breakdown

```
iPACE-CHIP Architecture
|
+-- Analog Front-End (AFE)
|   +-- Atrial sensing amplifier (programmable gain, bandwidth)
|   +-- Ventricular sensing amplifier (programmable gain, bandwidth)
|   +-- Bandpass filters (programmable cutoff)
|   +-- Auto-gain control
|   +-- Atrial output driver (programmable voltage/current)
|   +-- Ventricular output driver (programmable voltage/current)
|   +-- Lead impedance measurement circuit
|   +-- Charge pump (for high-voltage output from low-voltage supply)
|
+-- Digital Core
|   +-- Timing engine (all intervals: LRI, AVD, PVARP, etc.)
|   +-- Mode controller (VOO, VVI, AAI, DDD, DDDR)
|   +-- Sensing algorithms (auto-threshold, morphology discrimination)
|   +-- Pacing algorithms (capture management, rate smoothing)
|   +-- Arrhythmia detection (AF, VT rate discrimination)
|   +-- Rate response algorithm (accelerometer processing)
|   +-- State machine (device states: monitoring, pacing, charging, etc.)
|
+-- Telemetry and Communication
|   +-- RF transmitter/receiver (MICS band or BLE)
|   +-- Data encoding/decoding
|   +-- Bidirectional programming interface
|   +-- Patient alert circuit
|
+-- Power Management
|   +-- Low-dropout regulators (LDO)
|   +-- Battery monitoring (voltage, impedance)
|   +-- Charge pump for output
|   +-- Power-gating control
|   +-- Brownout detection
|   +-- Watchdog timer
|
+-- Memory
|   +-- EEPROM/FRAM (parameter storage)
|   +-- ROM (firmware)
|   +-- SRAM (working memory)
|
+-- Sensor Interface
|   +-- Accelerometer interface (SPI/I2C)
|   +-- Temperature sensor interface
|   +-- Minute ventilation interface (if applicable)
|
+-- Test and Debug
|   +-- Scan chain (DFT)
|   +-- Built-in self-test (BIST)
|   +-- JTAG interface
|   +-- Test mode control
```

## 12. Summary

IET-DAVV's role in the iPACE-CHIP project is multifaceted and critical:

| Role | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Implementation partner** | C2S program implementation at the institutional level |
| **Research hub** | Primary site for iPACE-CHIP design, development, and testing |
| **Training center** | Chip design training for students and professionals |
| **Innovation center** | Novel biomedical IC design techniques and IP creation |
| **Industry bridge** | Connecting academic research with industry needs |
| **Clinical liaison** | Bridging engineering development with clinical requirements |
| **Startup incubator** | Potential spawning of medical device startups |
| **Talent factory** | Producing industry-ready semiconductor engineers |

The combination of institutional infrastructure, research expertise (led by Dr. Vaibhav Neema and Dr. Ravi Sindal), C2S program support, and strategic positioning in central India makes IET-DAVV an ideal location for the development of India's first indigenous pacemaker chip.

The iPACE-CHIP project, housed at IET-DAVV, represents the realization of the C2S program's core vision: transforming India from a semiconductor design services provider into a creator of indigenous semiconductor products that address critical national needs — in this case, the development of affordable, accessible cardiac pacing technology for India and the world.
