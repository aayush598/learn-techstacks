# India's Semiconductor Mission and Ecosystem

## 1. Introduction

India's semiconductor journey represents one of the most significant technology policy initiatives in the nation's history. The **India Semiconductor Mission (ISM)**, launched in 2021 with an initial outlay of approximately Rs.76,000 crore for semiconductor fabrication and a total ecosystem investment exceeding Rs.1,00,000 crore, marks a decisive shift from India's traditional role as a semiconductor design and services hub to a comprehensive ecosystem encompassing design, fabrication, packaging, and product development.

For the iPACE-CHIP project, understanding the Indian semiconductor landscape is essential. This chapter provides a detailed examination of India's semiconductor mission, incentive schemes, fabrication investments, design ecosystem, and current state of the industry.

---

## 2. Historical Context of India's Semiconductor Industry

### 2.1 Early History (1960s-1980s)

| Year | Milestone | Significance |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| 1962 | Semiconductor Laboratory (SCL) established in Mohali | India's first semiconductor facility |
| 1965 | SCL produces first Indian-made integrated circuits | Proof of domestic capability |
| 1970s | SCL operates 8-inch wafer fab | Briefly competitive with global industry |
| 1984 | Electronics Policy of India | Electronics recognized as strategic sector |
| 1980s | Budget constraints limit SCL modernization | India falls behind global technology curve |

### 2.2 Design Era (1990s-2010s)

| Year | Milestone | Significance |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| 1990s | Multinational design centers established | India becomes a global design hub |
| 2000s | India chip design share grows to ~20% globally | Strong design capability established |
| 2010s | 100+ MNC design centers; 100,000+ design engineers | Mature design ecosystem |

### 2.3 The Semiconductor Gap

| Capability | India's Position | Gap Level |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------|
| **Chip design** | 20% global share | Minimal |
| **IP creation** | Growing but limited | Moderate |
| **Fabrication (fabs)** | None operational | Critical |
| **OSAT (packaging)** | Minimal domestic | Major |
| **Equipment manufacturing** | None | Complete |
| **Materials** | None | Complete |
| **Product development** | Mostly for foreign companies | Moderate |

## 3. India Semiconductor Mission (ISM)

### 3.1 Mission Structure

```
India Semiconductor Mission (ISM)
+-- Semiconductor Fabs Division
|   +-- Tata-PSMC (Dholera, Gujarat)
|   +-- ISMC (Hyderabad/Tumkur)
|   +-- CG Power (Assam)
+-- Display Fabs Division
+-- Compound Semiconductors Division
|   +-- Micron (Sanand, Gujarat)
|   +-- Other facilities
+-- Chip Design Division (C2S)
+-- Strategic Applications Division

```

| Component | Allocation (Rs. crore) | Approximate USD | Purpose |

| **Chip design (C2S)** | 3,000 | $375 million | Design ecosystem and startups |
| **Total** | ~1,06,100+ | ~$13+ billion | Comprehensive ecosystem |

## 4. Incentive Schemes

### 4.1 Semiconductor Fabrication Incentive Scheme

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Financial support** | Up to 50% of project cost for semiconductor fabs |
| **Maximum incentive** | Rs.5,000 crore per fab (for nodes less than or equal to 28nm) |
| **Duration** | 6 years from date of approval |
| **Eligibility** | Companies with demonstrated semiconductor manufacturing capability |

### 4.2 Compound Semiconductor Incentive Scheme

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Financial support** | Up to 50% of project cost |
| **Coverage** | SiC, GaN, GaAs, InP devices; photonic devices; OSAT facilities |
| **Eligibility** | Companies with relevant technology and manufacturing experience |

### 4.3 Design Linked Incentive (DLI) Scheme

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Financial support** | 50% of eligible expenditure (up to Rs.15 crore per year) |
| **Duration** | 5 years |
| **Eligibility** | Indian companies designing and marketing semiconductor chips |
| **Conditions** | Must be Indian-incorporated; Indian design team; Indian IP ownership |
| **Coverage** | Chip design, IP development, system design |

### 4.4 Incentive Summary for iPACE-CHIP

| Incentive Scheme | Relevance to iPACE-CHIP | Potential Benefit |
|-----------------|------------------------|-------------------|
| **DLI Scheme** | Direct applicability | 50% reimbursement of design costs |
| **C2S Program** | Direct applicability | EDA tools, training, fabrication funding |
| **ISM fab incentives** | Future benefit | Reduced fabrication costs |
| **Export incentives** | Future benefit | Benefits from EPCG scheme |

## 5. Fab Investments in India

### 5.1 Tata-PSMC Semiconductor Fab (Dholera, Gujarat)

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Location** | Dholera Special Investment Region, Gujarat |
| **Investment** | Rs.91,000 crore (~$11 billion USD) |
| **Technology nodes** | 28nm and below (roadmap to 14nm and below) |
| **Capacity** | 50,000 wafers per month (start) |
| **Partner** | PSMC (Powerchip, Taiwan) |
| **Expected production** | 2026-2027 |
| **Applications** | Consumer electronics, automotive, industrial, communications |

### 5.2 ISMC Semiconductor Fab

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Location** | Planned for Hyderabad/Tumkur region |
| **Investment** | ~$3 billion USD |
| **Technology nodes** | 65nm-180nm (mature nodes) |
| **Partner** | Tower Semiconductor (Israel) / Intel |
| **Applications** | Analog, mixed-signal, power management, automotive, medical |
| **Status** | Planning/regulatory approvals |

### 5.3 CG Power and Industrial Solutions (Assam)

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Location** | Assam |
| **Investment** | Rs.7,600 crore (~$1 billion USD) |
| **Technology** | OSAT (Outsourced Semiconductor Assembly and Test) |
| **Partner** | Renesas Electronics (Japan) |
| **Applications** | Advanced packaging, testing, assembly |

### 5.4 Micron Technology (Sanand, Gujarat)

| Parameter | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Location** | Sanand, Gujarat |
| **Investment** | $2.75 billion USD |
| **Technology** | OSAT - advanced memory packaging and testing |
| **Status** | Under construction; expected operational 2025 |

### 5.5 Fab Technology Relevance for iPACE-CHIP

| Fab | Technology Node | iPACE-CHIP Relevance |
|-----|----------------|---------------------|
| **Tata-PSMC** | 28nm and below | Advanced digital blocks |
| **ISMC/Tower** | 65nm-180nm | **Ideal** for analog/mixed-signal pacemaker IC |
| **CG Power** | OSAT/packaging | Pacemaker device packaging |

The ISMC/Tower fab (65nm-180nm) is most directly relevant for the iPACE-CHIP, as pacemaker ICs typically operate at these mature nodes where high-voltage circuits (5V-10V) are feasible, analog performance is optimal, and reliability models are well-established.

## 6. Make in India and Atmanirbhar Bharat

### 6.1 Make in India Initiative

Launched in September 2014, Make in India facilitates investment, fosters innovation, enhances skill development, and builds best-in-class manufacturing infrastructure.

**Relevance to semiconductor industry**:
- Encourages domestic manufacturing of electronics
- Provides regulatory simplification
- Offers infrastructure support (SEZs, industrial corridors)
- Facilitates foreign direct investment (FDI)

### 6.2 Atmanirbhar Bharat (Self-Reliant India)

Launched in May 2020, Atmanirbhar Bharat emphasizes self-reliance across five pillars:

| Pillar | Semiconductor Relevance |
|--------|----------------------|
| **Economy** | Building semiconductor self-sufficiency |
| **Infrastructure** | Fabs, OSAT facilities, design centers |
| **System** | Modernizing regulatory and policy frameworks |
| **Vibrant Demography** | Leveraging young, skilled workforce |
| **Demand** | 1.4 billion consumers creating massive domestic demand |

### 6.3 Strategic Importance

| Dimension | Importance |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Economic** | Semiconductor imports = $15+ billion annually |
| **Strategic** | Defense, space, communications require reliable supply |
| **Healthcare** | Medical devices require domestic capability |
| **Digital India** | IoT, 5G, AI all require semiconductor components |
| **Job creation** | Estimated 1 million+ direct jobs |
| **Supply chain resilience** | COVID-19 exposed vulnerabilities |

## 10. Challenges and Risks

### 10.1 Semiconductor Ecosystem Challenges

| Challenge | Status | Impact |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| **No operational fabs** | Under construction | Offshore fabrication required |
| **Limited analog design expertise** | Growing | Critical for medical ICs |
| **IP protection concerns** | Improving | May deter partnerships |
| **Skill gap** | Being addressed | Needs time |

### 10.2 Medical Device Challenges

| Challenge | Detail | Mitigation |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| **Long certification timeline** | CDSCO approval 1-3 years | Early engagement |
| **Clinical trial requirements** | Must demonstrate safety/efficacy | Partner with Indian hospitals |
| **Price sensitivity** | Very low costs required | Design for cost; high volume |
| **Quality perception** | Imported perceived as superior | Demonstrate equivalence |

## 11. Future Outlook

### 11.1 India Semiconductor Roadmap (2025-2035)

| Year | Expected Milestone |
|------|-------------------|
| **2025** | Micron OSAT operational; C2S first tape-outs |
| **2026** | First Indian fab (Tata-PSMC) begins production |
| **2027** | ISMC fab operational; first C2S products launched |
| **2028** | Multiple fabs operational; 100+ chip designs completed |
| **2030** | India reaches 5-10% of global semiconductor manufacturing |
| **2035** | Mature ecosystem; self-sufficient in key segments |

### 11.2 iPACE-CHIP in the National Context

The iPACE-CHIP project is positioned to be:
1. One of the first medical device chips designed in India under C2S
2. A demonstration project for indigenous biomedical IC development
3. A potential export product for low-cost pacemaker technology
4. A catalyst for building medical device semiconductor capabilities
5. A contribution to India's healthcare sovereignty goals
