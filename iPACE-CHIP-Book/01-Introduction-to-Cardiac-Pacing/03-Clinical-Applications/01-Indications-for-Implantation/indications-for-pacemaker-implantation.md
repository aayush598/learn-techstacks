# Indications for Pacemaker Implantation

## 1. Introduction

The decision to implant a permanent cardiac pacemaker is one of the most consequential clinical judgments in cardiology. It requires the integration of patient symptoms, electrocardiographic findings, electrophysiological data, comorbidities, and evidence from randomized clinical trials — all weighed against the risks of the procedure and the long-term consequences of device therapy. For the developer of the iPACE-CHIP, understanding these indications is essential: the device must be designed to address the full spectrum of clinical scenarios where pacing is indicated, and its capabilities must align with the therapeutic requirements of each indication.

This chapter provides a comprehensive review of the indications for permanent pacemaker implantation, organized by arrhythmia category and classified according to the American College of Cardiology/American Heart Association (ACC/AHA) and European Society of Cardiology (ESC) evidence-based classification systems.

---

## 2. Classification of Recommendations and Evidence Levels

### 2.1 ACC/AHA Classification of Recommendations

| Class | Description | Recommendation |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| **Class I** | Benefit >>> Risk | Procedure/treatment SHOULD be performed/administered |
| **Class II** | Benefit ≥ Risk | Procedure/treatment MAY be considered |
| **Class IIa** | Benefit >> Risk | It is reasonable to perform/administer |
| **Class IIb** | Benefit ≥ Risk | Procedure/treatment MAY BE considered |
| **Class III** | Benefit ≤ Risk or no benefit | Procedure/treatment SHOULD NOT be performed/administered |

### 2.2 ACC/AHA Evidence Levels

| Level | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Level A** | Data derived from multiple randomized clinical trials |
| **Level B** | Data derived from a single randomized trial or non-randomized studies |
| **Level C** | Consensus opinion of experts, case studies, standard of care |

### 2.3 ESC Classification (Similar)

The ESC uses a similar classification (Class I, IIa, IIb, III) with evidence levels A, B, C.

## 3. Sinus Node Dysfunction (SND)

Sinus node dysfunction encompasses a spectrum of SA node abnormalities and is the most common indication for permanent pacemaker implantation in developed countries (approximately 40–50% of all pacemaker implants).

### 3.1 Class I Indications (Strong Recommendation)

| Indication | Evidence Level | Rationale |
|-----------|---------------|-----------|
| SND with documented symptomatic bradycardia (sinus bradycardia, sinus arrest, SA exit block) | B | Direct evidence from observational studies; symptoms relieved by pacing |
| SND with documented symptomatic bradycardia caused by essential drug therapy where no alternative drug is available | C | Iatrogenic bradycardia; pacing allows continuation of necessary medications |
| SND with chronotropic incompetence and symptoms | B | Inability to increase heart rate with exercise; rate-responsive pacing indicated |

### 3.2 Class IIa Indications (Reasonable)

| Indication | Evidence Level | Rationale |
|-----------|---------------|-----------|
| SND with heart rate < 40 bpm, even in the absence of a clear temporal correlation with symptoms | C | Significant bradycardia; high risk of symptomatic episodes |
| SND with syncope of uncertain etiology, when other significant causes have been excluded and electrophysiological study demonstrates sinus node dysfunction | C | High probability that pacing will prevent recurrent syncope |

### 3.3 Class IIb Indications (May Be Considered)

| Indication | Evidence Level | Rationale |
|-----------|---------------|-----------|
| Minimal symptoms and heart rate 40–50 bpm | C | May not benefit from pacing; watchful waiting may be appropriate |
| SND with asymptomatic bradycardia and heart rate < 40 bpm | C | Pacing may prevent future symptomatic episodes |

### 3.4 Class III Indications (Not Recommended)

| Indication | Evidence Level | Rationale |
|-----------|---------------|-----------|
| Asymptomatic SND with heart rate > 40 bpm | C | No indication for pacing; risk of procedure outweighs benefit |
| SND with asymptomatic bradycardia due to reversible causes (e.g., hypothyroidism, medications that can be withdrawn) | C | Treat the underlying cause; pacing not indicated |
| SND with symptoms clearly attributable to a non-cardiac cause | C | Pacing will not address the symptoms |

## 4. Atrioventricular Conduction Disease

AV conduction disease encompasses a spectrum from first-degree AV block to complete (third-degree) AV block, and is the second most common indication for permanent pacemaker implantation.

### 4.1 Third-Degree (Complete) AV Block

#### 4.1.1 Class I Indications

| Indication | Evidence Level | Rationale |
|-----------|---------------|-----------|
| Complete AV block with symptomatic bradycardia | B | Life-threatening; pacing is the definitive treatment |
| Complete AV block with documented asystole ≥ 3 seconds or escape rate < 40 bpm, with or without symptoms | B | High risk of syncope and sudden death |
| Complete AV block after catheter ablation of the AV junction | B | Iatrogenic; complete dependence on escape rhythm |
| Complete AV block with neuromuscular diseases (e.g., myotonic muscular dystrophy, Kearns-Sayre syndrome, Erb dystrophy, peroneal muscular atrophy) with or without symptoms | B | Progressive conduction disease; high risk of sudden death |
| Complete AV block with ventricular rates > 40 bpm but with cardiomegaly or left ventricular dysfunction | C | Even "adequate" escape rate insufficient for hemodynamic needs |

#### 4.1.2 Class IIa Indications

| Indication | Evidence Level | Rationale |
|-----------|---------------|-----------|
| Asymptomatic complete AV block with ventricular escape rate > 40 bpm (in awake patients) | C | Low risk of asystole in short term but may progress |
| Asymptomatic Mobitz Type II with narrow QRS | B | Risk of progression to complete heart block |
| Asymptomatic complete AV block in awake patients with HR > 40 bpm but < 50 bpm during sleep | C | May progress during wakefulness |

#### 4.1.3 Class III Indications

| Indication | Evidence Level | Rationale |
|-----------|---------------|-----------|
| Asymptomatic complete AV block with escape rate > 50 bpm | C | Low risk; observation may be appropriate |
| AV block due to reversible causes (e.g., drugs, Lyme disease, elevated vagal tone) | C | Treat underlying cause; pacing not indicated if reversible |

### 4.2 Mobitz Type II AV Block

| Class | Indication | Evidence |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| **I** | Mobitz Type II with wide QRS and symptoms | B |
| **I** | Asymptomatic Mobitz Type II with wide QRS | B |
| **IIa** | Asymptomatic Mobitz Type II with narrow QRS | B |

### 4.3 First-Degree AV Block

| Class | Indication | Evidence |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| **III** | First-degree AV block with symptoms caused by the prolonged PR interval (e.g., pacemaker-like symptoms, heart failure due to loss of atrial contribution) | C |

### 4.4 Bilateral Bundle Branch Block

| Class | Indication | Evidence |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| **I** | Alternating bundle branch block (RBBB + LAFB or RBBB + LPFB) | B |
| **IIa** | Syncope of uncertain etiology with HV interval > 100 ms during EPS | B |
| **IIa** | Incidental finding of bifascicular block (RBBB + LAFB or RBBB + LPFB) without symptoms, but with prolonged HV interval (> 100 ms) at EPS | B |
| **IIb** | Neuromuscular disease (e.g., myotonic dystrophy) with bifascicular block, even without symptoms | C |

## 5. Specific Pacing Indications

### 5.1 Hypersensitive Carotid Sinus Syndrome and Neurocardiogenic Syncope

#### 5.1.1 Carotid Sinus Hypersensitivity

**Definition**: Exaggerated cardiovascular response to carotid sinus stimulation:
- **Cardioinhibitory response**: Asystole > 3 seconds or sinus rate < 40 bpm
- **Vasodepressor response**: Systolic BP drop > 50 mmHg without significant heart rate change
- **Mixed response**: Both cardioinhibitory and vasodepressor components

| Class | Indication | Evidence |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| **I** | Recurrent syncope caused by documented significant cardioinhibitory response to carotid sinus stimulation (asystole > 3 seconds) | B |
| **IIa** | Recurrent syncope with mixed cardioinhibitory and vasodepressor response | B |
| **IIb** | Neurocardiogenic syncope with significant cardioinhibitory response but without clear documentation of a temporal relationship to symptoms | C |

#### 5.1.2 Neurocardiogenic (Vasovagal) Syncope

| Class | Indication | Evidence |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| **IIb** | Recurrent severe neurocardiogenic syncope with documented significant cardioinhibitory response | C |
| **III** | Neurocardiogenic syncope without cardioinhibitory response | B |
| **IIa** | Tilt-table test-positive syncope with pronounced cardioinhibition (asystole > 3 seconds) and frequent recurrences | B |

**Important note**: Pacing alone has limited efficacy in vasodepressor-predominant syncope (because it does not address the hypotension component). Rate-smoothing and specialized algorithms (e.g., closed-loop stimulation) may provide additional benefit.

### 5.2 Post-Myocardial Infarction Conduction Disturbances

| Class | Indication | Evidence | Rationale |
|-------|-----------|----------|-----------|
| **I** | High-degree AV block (Mobitz Type II or complete) with symptomatic bradycardia persistent > 7 days after acute MI | B | Prognostic significance of persistent post-MI AV block |
| **I** | High-degree AV block with alternating bundle branch block | B | Extensive conduction system disease |
| **IIa** | Persistent advanced AV block (2nd or 3rd degree) with bilateral bundle branch block after acute MI, even without symptoms | C | Risk of progression to complete heart block |

### 5.3 Post-Cardiac Surgery

| Class | Indication | Evidence |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| **I** | Post-operative high-degree AV block (> 7 days) not expected to resolve | B |
| **I** | Symptomatic bradycardia after cardiac surgery requiring pacing | C |
| **IIa** | Post-operative transient AV block that resolves but recurs or is associated with bundle branch block | C |

### 5.4 Hypertrophic Cardiomyopathy (HCM)

| Class | Indication | Evidence |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| **IIb** | Symptomatic HCM patients with LVOT obstruction who are not candidates for septal myectomy/myolysis or alcohol septal ablation, and in whom drug therapy has failed | B |
| **IIb** | Dual-chamber pacing with short AV delay to reduce LVOT gradient | B |

**Note**: The role of pacing in HCM has diminished with the development of more effective therapies (septal ablation, myectomy, mavacamten). Pacing is now a last resort for LVOT gradient reduction.

### 5.5 Cardiac Transplant Recipients

| Class | Indication | Evidence |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| **I** | Symptomatic SND with or without conduction disease (post-transplant) | C |
| **IIa** | SND occurring > 1 year after transplant | C |

### 5.6 Congenital Heart Disease

| Class | Indication | Evidence |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| **I** | Post-operative high-degree AV block persisting > 7 days | B |
| **I** | Congenital third-degree AV block with wide QRS escape, ventricular dysfunction, or symptomatic bradycardia | B |
| **IIa** | Congenital third-degree AV block with narrow QRS and stable escape rate, but with significant ventricular rate variation or exercise intolerance | B |
| **IIa** | Congenital heart disease patients with asymptomatic SND and heart rate < 40 bpm or pauses > 3 seconds | C |
| **IIb** | Congenital third-degree AV block in asymptomatic infants/children with narrow QRS, stable rate > 50 bpm, and normal ventricular function | C |

## 6. Pacing for Prevention of Tachyarrhythmias

### 6.1 Atrial Pacing to Prevent Atrial Fibrillation

| Class | Indication | Evidence |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| **IIa** | Dual-site or biatrial pacing in patients with drug-refractory atrial fibrillation who have undergone pacemaker implantation for another indication | B |
| **IIb** | Atrial overdrive pacing to prevent atrial fibrillation | B |

### 6.2 Pacing for Tachycardia Termination

| Class | Indication | Evidence |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| **IIa** | Antitachycardia pacing (ATP) in patients with AVNRT or AVRT who have an implanted pacemaker for another indication | C |
| **IIa** | Pacemaker implantation in patients with implantable defibrillator and recurrent VT requiring frequent ATP or cardioversion | B |

## 7. Implantable Cardioverter-Defibrillator (ICD) Indications

While ICD implantation is a separate topic from pacemaker implantation, many patients receiving ICDs also benefit from pacemaker functions. The iPACE-CHIP should understand these indications as it may be deployed in CRT-P or ICD-like systems.

### 7.1 Primary Prevention of Sudden Cardiac Death

| Class | Indication | Evidence |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| **I** | Prior MI, LVEF ≤ 35%, NYHA II-III, on optimal medical therapy, with documented NSVT and inducible VT at EPS | A |
| **I** | Non-ischemic cardiomyopathy, LVEF ≤ 35%, NYHA II-III, on optimal medical therapy, with inducible VT/VF at EPS | B |
| **IIa** | Ischemic cardiomyopathy, LVEF ≤ 30%, NYHA I, on optimal medical therapy (no EPS needed) | A |
| **IIa** | Non-ischemic cardiomyopathy, LVEF ≤ 30%, NYHA I, on optimal medical therapy | B |

### 7.2 Secondary Prevention

| Class | Indication | Evidence |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| **I** | Cardiac arrest due to VT/VF not attributable to a reversible cause | A |
| **I** | Spontaneous sustained VT in patients with structural heart disease | A |
| **I** | Syncope of undetermined origin with clinically relevant ventricular arrhythmias induced at EPS | B |

## 8. CRT Indications

### 8.1 CRT-P (Biventricular Pacing Without ICD)

| Class | Indication | Evidence |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| **I** | LVEF ≤ 35%, sinus rhythm, LBBB with QRS ≥ 150 ms, NYHA II-IV (ambulatory) on optimal medical therapy, with CRT implantation by a team experienced in CRT | A |
| **I** | LVEF ≤ 35%, sinus rhythm, non-LBBB with QRS ≥ 150 ms, NYHA II-IV on optimal medical therapy | A |
| **IIa** | LVEF ≤ 35%, sinus rhythm, LBBB with QRS 120–149 ms, NYHA II-IV on optimal medical therapy | B |
| **IIa** | LVEF ≤ 35%, atrial fibrillation patients who require ventricular pacing (or have > 40% V pacing) and meet other CRT criteria | B |
| **IIa** | LVEF ≤ 35%, NYHA III-IV on optimal medical therapy, with device-dependent (> 90% V pacing) | C |

### 8.2 CRT-D (Combined CRT + ICD)

| Class | Indication | Evidence |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| **I** | Same as CRT-P Class I indications, with concurrent ICD indication | A |
| **IIa** | Same as CRT-P Class IIa indications, with concurrent ICD indication | B |

### 8.3 CRT Implantation in Specific Populations

| Population | Recommendation | Evidence |
|-----------|---------------|----------|
| **Elderly (≥ 75 years)** | CRT recommended if other criteria met | B |
| **Women** | CRT recommended; may benefit more than men | A |
| **QRS 120–149 ms (non-LBBB)** | CRT may be less beneficial; IIb recommendation | B |
| **QRS < 120 ms** | CRT NOT recommended (Class III) | A |
| **Right bundle branch block** | CRT less beneficial; III recommendation | A |

## 9. Special Populations

### 9.1 Pediatric and Congenital Heart Disease Patients

| Indication | Class | Evidence |
|-----------|-------|----------|
| Post-operative complete heart block > 7 days | I | B |
| Congenital complete heart block with symptoms, wide QRS, or low rate | I | B |
| Congenital complete heart block without symptoms but with evidence of hemodynamic compromise | IIa | B |
| Post-surgical sinus node dysfunction | I | C |
| Drug-refractory atrial tachyarrhythmias with need for AV block | IIa | B |

### 9.2 Patients with Neuromuscular Diseases

| Disease | Conduction Abnormality | Pacing Indication |
|---------|----------------------|-------------------|
| **Myotonic muscular dystrophy** | Progressive AV block (fascicular → complete) | I if bifascicular block or high-degree block |
| **Kearns-Sayre syndrome** | Complete AV block, bundle branch blocks | I; early implantation recommended |
| **Erb dystrophy** | AV block, conduction delays | IIa |
| **Peroneal muscular atrophy** | AV block, SND | I if symptomatic |
| **Myasthenia gravis** | Rare conduction involvement | Rarely indicated |

### 9.3 Patients with Device-Induced Cardiomyopathy

**Pacing-induced cardiomyopathy (PICM)**: Patients with chronic RV pacing who develop cardiomyopathy (LVEF reduction to ≤ 40%) and symptoms.

| Management Strategy | Approach |
|---------------------|---------|
| **Upgrade to CRT** | Biventricular pacing to resynchronize |
| **Upgrade to His-bundle/LBBAP pacing** | More physiological activation |
| **Prevention** | Minimize RV pacing (long AV delay, mode switching, CRT in high V-pacing percentage) |

## 10. Indications NOT Recommended

### 10.1 Class III (Not Recommended)

| Indication | Rationale |
|-----------|-----------|
| Asymptomatic bradycardia (heart rate > 50 bpm) | No benefit; risk of procedure |
| Drug-induced bradycardia (when drug can be discontinued or changed) | Treat underlying cause |
| Asymptomatic SND with heart rate > 40 bpm | Observation appropriate |
| Tachyarrhythmia control with pacemaker when alternative therapy is available | Less invasive alternatives preferred |
| Inappropriate ventricular rates during AF that could be controlled by AV node ablation and pacemaker, if the patient prefers the alternative approach | Shared decision-making |
| Pacing for prevention of recurrent vasovagal syncope without documented cardioinhibitory response | Limited efficacy |

## 11. Summary of Pacing Indications by NBG Mode

### 11.1 Mode Selection by Indication

| Indication | Recommended Mode | Alternative Mode |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------------|
| **SND, intact AV conduction** | AAI(R) or DDD(R) | — |
| **SND + AV block** | DDD(R) | VVIR (if atrial lead not feasible) |
| **Isolated AV block (high-degree)** | DDD(R) | VVIR (elderly, limited activity) |
| **Chronic AF + AV block** | VVIR | — |
| **Hypersensitive carotid sinus (cardioinhibitory)** | DDD(R) | — |
| **Neurocardiogenic syncope (cardioinhibitory)** | DDD(R) or VVIR | — |
| **HCM (LVOT obstruction)** | DDD (short AV delay) | — |
| **CRT indication** | DDD(R) with biventricular pacing | — |
| **Post-MI AV block** | DDD(R) | VVIR |

### 11.2 Summary of Global Pacemaker Implantation Indications

| Indication | Approximate % of All Implants | Trend |
|-----------|------------------------------|-------|
| **Sick sinus syndrome** | 40–50% | Increasing (aging population) |
| **AV block (complete, Mobitz II)** | 30–40% | Stable |
| **Cardiac resynchronization** | 10–15% | Increasing |
| **Other (ICD, neurocardiogenic, etc.) | 5–10% | Increasing |

## 12. Guidelines for iPACE-CHIP Design

Understanding the full spectrum of pacing indications informs the iPACE-CHIP design requirements:

| Indication Category | Minimum iPACE-CHIP Capability | Aspirational Capability |
|--------------------|-------------------------------|----------------------|
| **SND (AAIR/AAI)** | Single-chamber atrial pacing, rate-responsive | — |
| **AV block (VVI/VVIR)** | Single-chamber ventricular pacing, rate-responsive | — |
| **SND + AV block (DDDR)** | Dual-chamber pacing and sensing, rate-responsive | Full DDD/DDDR timing cycles |
| **AF with AV block (VVIR)** | VVIR pacing | Mode switching algorithms |
| **Neurocardiogenic syncope** | DDD with rate-drop response | Closed-loop stimulation |
| **HCM** | DDD with programmable AV delay | — |
| **CRT indication** | Three-chamber (biventricular) pacing | CRT-P with optimization algorithms |
| **ICD indication** | Not primary pacemaker function | Future: integrated pacing + defibrillation |

The iPACE-CHIP must support at minimum VVI/VVIR and ideally DDD/DDDR functionality to address the majority of pacing indications. Rate-responsive capability (at least accelerometer-based) is essential for optimizing patient outcomes across all age groups and activity levels.
