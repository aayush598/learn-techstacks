# Analog Performance Measurement

## 15.6.1 Overview

The analog front-end (AFE) of the iPACE-CHIP is the most critical subsystem from a signal integrity perspective. It must faithfully amplify cardiac signals in the microvolt range while rejecting noise sources that can be orders of magnitude larger than the signal of interest. This chapter describes the comprehensive measurement methodology used to characterize every analog parameter of the iPACE-CHIP, from the sensing amplifier's noise floor to the pacing output driver's voltage accuracy. These measurements determine whether the analog performance meets the requirements for reliable cardiac sensing and therapy delivery.

## 15.6.2 Measurement Environment

### Shielded Enclosure

All sensitive analog measurements are performed inside a shielded enclosure (Faraday cage) to eliminate electromagnetic interference. The enclosure specifications are:

```
Shielding: Mu-metal + copper laminate
Attenuation: > 80 dB at 50/60 Hz, > 60 dB at 1 MHz
Dimensions: 60 cm x 40 cm x 40 cm (internal)
Entry: Filtered connectors for power, signals, and control
Grounding: Single-point ground to laboratory earth
```

### Low-Noise Power Supply

The analog supply (VDD_ANA) is provided by a battery-powered low-noise LDO to avoid mains-borne noise:

```
Supply: 2x 1.5V AA batteries in series (3.0V)
Regulator: TPS7A20 (10 uV RMS output noise)
Filter: Pi-filter with 100 uF electrolytic + 10 uF ceramic + 100 nF ceramic
Regulated output: 2.500V
Ripple: < 1 uV RMS at 100 Hz bandwidth
```

### Signal Source

Test signals are generated by a battery-powered function generator with sub-microvolt output capability:

```
Generator: Stanford Research Systems DS360 (ultra-low distortion)
Coupling: Transformer-coupled for galvanic isolation
Attenuator: Custom resistive divider (1:1000 ratio)
Output impedance: 10 kohm (matching expected electrode impedance)
```

## 15.6.3 Sensing Amplifier Characterization

### Input-Referred Noise

The noise floor of the sensing amplifier is the most fundamental analog parameter. It determines the minimum detectable cardiac signal.

```
Noise Measurement Procedure:
  1. Short both AFE inputs together (differential input = 0)
  2. Set PGA to maximum gain (x16)
  3. Sample ADC output at 1 kHz for 60 seconds (60,000 samples)
  4. Apply Hanning window
  5. Compute FFT magnitude spectrum
  6. Integrate noise power over 0.5-200 Hz bandwidth
  7. Calculate RMS noise = sqrt(integrated power)
  8. Refer to input: Vnoise_input = Vnoise_output / PGA_gain

Results:
  PGA Gain | Vnoise_output (uV RMS) | Vnoise_input (uV RMS)
  x1       | 62.4                   | 62.4
  x2       | 38.5                   | 19.3
  x4       | 22.1                   | 5.5
  x8       | 14.8                   | 1.85
  x16      | 10.2                   | 0.64

Input-referred noise at maximum gain: 0.64 uV RMS
This is well below the 5 uV RMS specification.
```

### Noise Spectral Density

The noise spectral density provides insight into the noise sources:

```
Frequency (Hz) | Noise Density (nV/sqrt(Hz))
---------------|-----------------------------
0.5            | 450
1              | 320
5              | 180
10             | 140
50             | 120
100            | 115
200            | 110

The 1/f corner frequency is approximately 5 Hz, where the flicker noise
equals the thermal noise floor of ~110 nV/sqrt(Hz).
```

### CMRR (Common-Mode Rejection Ratio)

```
CMRR Measurement:
  1. Apply 100 mV peak sine wave (50 Hz) to both inputs (common-mode)
  2. Set PGA to gain of x8
  3. Measure differential output amplitude
  4. CMRR = 20 * log10(Vcm_applied / Vout_diff)

Results by Frequency:
  Frequency (Hz) | CMRR (dB)
  1              | 92
  10             | 90
  50             | 86
  60             | 85
  100            | 82
  200            | 78

CMRR > 80 dB across the 0.5-200 Hz band of interest.
The slight degradation at higher frequencies is due to capacitor mismatch
in the feedback network.
```

### PSRR (Power Supply Rejection Ratio)

```
PSRR Measurement:
  1. Inject 100 mV peak sine wave on VDD_ANA
  2. Sweep injection frequency from 1 Hz to 1 MHz
  3. Measure output referred noise
  4. Calculate PSRR

Results:
  Frequency (Hz) | PSRR (dB)
  1              | 88
  10             | 85
  100            | 78
  1k             | 65
  10k            | 48
  100k           | 32

At 50/60 Hz, PSRR > 78 dB, providing adequate rejection of power supply noise.
```

## 15.6.4 ADC Characterization

### Static Performance (DNL/INL)

```
ADC Linearity Test:
  1. Apply DC voltage ramp from -5 mV to +5 mV (differential)
  2. Sample at 256 Hz with 16x oversampling
  3. Collect 100 samples at each of 4096 voltage steps
  4. Build histogram of ADC codes
  5. Calculate DNL and INL from histogram

Results:
  DNL:
    Maximum DNL: +0.65 LSB / -0.58 LSB
    Occurs at: Code 0x800 (mid-scale)
    
  INL:
    Maximum INL: +1.2 LSB / -1.1 LSB
    Shape: Mild bow (second-order nonlinearity)
    INL < 2 LSB across full range (meets specification)

Differential Nonlinearity Plot (simplified):
  Code    | DNL (LSB)
  0x000   | +0.02
  0x200   | +0.15
  0x400   | +0.32
  0x600   | +0.48
  0x800   | +0.65
  0xA00   | +0.42
  0xC00   | +0.18
  0xE00   | +0.05
  0xFFF   | -0.12
```

### Dynamic Performance

```
Dynamic Test Setup:
  1. Apply 1 mV peak sine wave at 40 Hz (mid-band frequency)
  2. Sample at 1 kHz (oversampled)
  3. Capture 16,384 samples (16.384 seconds)
  4. Apply Hanning window
  5. Compute FFT
  6. Calculate SINAD, THD, SFDR, ENOB

Results:
  Signal frequency: 40 Hz
  Sample rate: 1 kHz
  FFT bins: 16,384
  
  SINAD: 72.5 dB
  THD: -78.2 dB (dominated by 2nd harmonic at -82 dB)
  SFDR: 78.2 dB
  ENOB = (SINAD - 1.76) / 6.02 = 11.75 bits
  
  Effective resolution: 11.75 bits (from nominal 12-bit ADC)
  This is adequate for cardiac signal digitization.
```

### ADC Settling Time

```
Step Response Test:
  1. Apply 1 mV step to ADC input (using fast switch)
  2. Record ADC output at maximum sample rate
  3. Measure time to within 0.5 LSB of final value

Results:
  Settling time (10% to 90%): 8.5 us
  Settling time (to 0.5 LSB): 15.2 us
  Overshoot: 0.3% of step height
  Ringing: < 1 cycle at 150 kHz natural frequency
  
  This settling time allows ADC conversion within the 100 us conversion window.
```

## 15.6.5 Programmable Gain Amplifier

### Gain Accuracy

```
Gain Accuracy Test:
  1. Apply 100 uV input signal
  2. Set PGA to each gain setting
  3. Measure output amplitude
  4. Calculate actual gain = Vout / Vin
  5. Calculate error from nominal gain

Results:
  Nominal Gain | Actual Gain | Error (%)
  x1           | 1.008       | +0.8%
  x2           | 2.015       | +0.75%
  x4           | 4.032       | +0.8%
  x8           | 8.064       | +0.8%
  x16          | 16.19       | +1.19%

All gains within 2% of nominal (meets specification).
The slight positive error suggests systematic offset in the gain resistor
trimming, which can be corrected in calibration.
```

### Gain Switching Transient

```
Transient Measurement:
  1. Set PGA to x4
  2. Apply 100 uV DC signal
  3. Switch PGA to x8 (trigger on gain change command)
  4. Measure output settling time

Results:
  Output spike magnitude: 15 mV (duration: 2 us)
  Settling time to 1 LSB: 12 us
  Gain change command to valid output: 15 us
  
  The output spike is safely blanked by the AFE controller during
  gain transitions, preventing false sense events.
```

## 15.6.6 Pacing Output Driver

### Voltage Accuracy

```
Output Voltage Test:
  1. Configure pacing output amplitude
  2. Deliver pacing pulse into 500 ohm load (simulating tissue)
  3. Measure output voltage with oscilloscope
  4. Repeat for all amplitude settings

Results:
  Setting (V) | Measured (V) | Error (%)
  1.0         | 0.99         | -1.0%
  1.5         | 1.49         | -0.67%
  2.0         | 1.98         | -1.0%
  2.5         | 2.48         | -0.8%
  3.0         | 2.97         | -1.0%
  3.5         | 3.46         | -1.14%
  4.0         | 3.96         | -1.0%
  5.0         | 4.94         | -1.2%
  6.0         | 5.92         | -1.33%
  7.0         | 6.90         | -1.43%
  7.5         | 7.38         | -1.6%

All outputs within 2% of setting. The slight negative error is due to
the output driver's finite on-resistance (approximately 50 ohm).
```

### Pulse Width Accuracy

```
Pulse Width Test:
  1. Configure pulse width
  2. Deliver pacing pulse
  3. Measure time from 50% amplitude rising edge to 50% falling edge

Results:
  Setting (ms) | Measured (ms) | Error (%)
  0.05         | 0.049         | -2.0%
  0.10         | 0.098         | -2.0%
  0.25         | 0.246         | -1.6%
  0.50         | 0.494         | -1.2%
  1.00         | 0.990         | -1.0%
  1.50         | 1.488         | -0.8%
  2.00         | 1.982         | -0.9%

All pulse widths within 2% of setting, adequate for pacing therapy.
```

### Output Impedance

```
Output Impedance Test:
  1. Configure pacing output at 3.5V, 0.5 ms
  2. Measure output voltage with no load (open circuit)
  3. Attach 500 ohm load, measure output voltage
  4. Calculate output impedance: Rout = Rload * (Vopen/Vloaded - 1)

Results:
  Rout = 500 * (3.52 / 3.46 - 1) = 500 * 0.0173 = 8.7 ohm

  The output driver has very low output impedance, which is desirable
  for maintaining voltage accuracy across varying lead impedances.
```

### Charge Balancing

```
Charge Balance Test:
  1. Configure pacing output at 3.5V, 0.5 ms, tip-positive
  2. Measure output current through 500 ohm load
  3. Integrate current over full pulse duration (charge delivered)
  4. Verify cathodic (passive) phase integral matches anodic phase

Results:
  Cathodic charge: 1.75 uC (3.5V * 0.5ms / 500 ohm * 0.5ms)
  Actually: Q_cathodic = (3.5V / 500ohm) * 0.5ms = 7mA * 0.5ms = 3.5 uC
  
  The iPACE-CHIP uses asymmetric charge balancing:
  Active phase: 3.5 uC (cathodic, at pacing voltage)
  Recovery phase: -3.5 uC (anodic, at 1/10 voltage, 10x duration)
  
  Net charge after recovery: < 0.1 uC residual (adequate for safety)
```

## 15.6.7 Reference Voltage

### Bandgap Reference Accuracy

```
Bandgap Reference Test:
  1. Measure VREF output voltage at 25C
  2. Sweep temperature from -40C to +85C
  3. Record VREF at each temperature point
  4. Calculate initial accuracy and temperature coefficient

Results:
  Nominal VREF: 1.200 V
  Measured VREF at 25C: 1.2018 V (+0.15% error)
  
  Temperature Coefficient:
  -40C:  1.2045 V
  -20C:  1.2035 V
   0C:   1.2028 V
  25C:   1.2018 V
  50C:   1.2005 V
  70C:   1.1995 V
  85C:   1.1988 V
  
  Temperature coefficient: -25 ppm/C (within 50 ppm/C specification)
  Total variation over -40C to +85C: 5.7 mV (0.475%)
```

### Reference Noise

```
Reference Noise Measurement:
  1. Connect VREF output to low-noise amplifier (gain x1000)
  2. Record amplified noise on oscilloscope
  3. Calculate input-referred noise
  4. Perform FFT to characterize spectral content

Results:
  Broadband noise (0.5-200 Hz): 3.2 uV RMS
  1/f corner: 8 Hz
  White noise floor: 12 nV/sqrt(Hz)
  Peak at 50 Hz: 0.5 uV (mains pickup despite shielding)
```

## 15.6.8 Input Protection Verification

### Overvoltage Survival

```
Overvoltage Test:
  1. Apply increasing voltage to AFE inputs
  2. Monitor input current (should be limited by protection circuit)
  3. Verify AFE function after removal of overvoltage
  4. Repeat for negative overvoltage

Results:
  Applied Voltage | Input Current | Post-Recovery Function
  +1V             | 1.2 mA        | Normal
  +2V             | 2.4 mA        | Normal
  +5V             | 5.8 mA        | Normal
  +10V            | 11.5 mA       | Normal
  -1V             | 1.1 mA        | Normal
  -2V             | 2.3 mA        | Normal
  -5V             | 5.5 mA        | Normal
  
  Protection diodes clamp at approximately +/- 0.6V above/below supply.
  Input current is limited by the 1 kohm series resistors.
  AFE fully recovers after overvoltage removal.
```

### Defibrillator Pulses

```
Defibrillator Pulse Test (IEC 60601-2-4):
  1. Apply 5 kV, 4 ms monophasic defibrillator pulse to inputs
  2. Verify input protection prevents damage to AFE
  3. Verify chip function is preserved after pulse
  4. Check that only the pacing output enters high-impedance state
     (as required by safety standard)

Results:
  Input current during pulse: 3.2 A (peak, clamped by TVS)
  AFE function: Preserved (within 1 ms of pulse end)
  Pacing output: High-impedance during pulse (as designed)
  Recovery time: 2.5 ms after pulse end
  Residual voltage at input: < 100 mV within 5 ms
```

## 15.6.9 Analog Crosstalk

### Channel-to-Channel Crosstalk

```
Crosstalk Measurement:
  1. Apply 10 mV sine wave to AIN1
  2. Measure output on AIN2 (should be zero)
  3. Calculate crosstalk = 20*log10(Vout2/Vin1)
  4. Repeat for all channel pairs

Results:
  Source -> Victim | Crosstalk (dB) | Frequency
  AIN1 -> AIN2     | -72            | 40 Hz
  AIN1 -> AIN3     | -68            | 40 Hz
  AIN2 -> AIN3     | -70            | 40 Hz
  AIN1 -> VOUT     | -65            | 40 Hz
  
  All crosstalk values better than -60 dB, adequate for cardiac sensing.
```

### Digital-to-Analog Crosstalk

```
Dig-to-Analog Test:
  1. Toggle all digital outputs at 1 MHz
  2. Measure AFE output noise
  3. Compare with baseline (digital quiet) noise

Results:
  Baseline noise: 0.64 uV RMS
  With digital activity: 0.72 uV RMS
  Noise increase: 0.08 uV RMS (12.5%)
  
  The noise increase from digital activity is minimal, indicating
  effective on-chip isolation between digital and analog domains.
```

## 15.6.10 Analog Timing Parameters

### ADC Conversion Timing

```
ADC Timing:
  Start-of-conversion to end-of-conversion: 95 us
  Sample-and-hold acquisition time: 15 us
  Conversion time (12-bit SAR): 80 us
  Data ready interrupt: 2 us after conversion complete
  
  This timing supports a 256 Hz sampling rate (3.9 ms per cycle),
  which is adequate for the 0.5-200 Hz cardiac signal bandwidth.
```

### AFE Settling Time

```
AFE Channel Settling:
  Channel switching time: 50 us
  PGA gain change settling: 15 us
  Total channel change to valid sample: 65 us
  
  With 4 channels, total sampling cycle: 4 * 65 us = 260 us
  Effective per-channel sample rate: ~384 Hz (adequate for Nyquist)
```

## 15.6.11 Summary

The analog performance characterization of the iPACE-CHIP demonstrates that the analog front-end meets all specifications for cardiac sensing and pacing. The input-referred noise of 0.64 uV RMS at maximum gain provides ample margin below the 5 uV specification. The ADC achieves 11.75 effective bits of resolution with good linearity. The pacing output driver provides accurate voltage and pulse width control across the full output range. The reference voltage meets accuracy and stability requirements across the operating temperature range. These measurements form the basis for the product datasheet and provide the evidence required for regulatory submissions demonstrating analog performance compliance.
