# BIS Standards for iPACE-CHIP

## 1. Introduction to BIS Standards

The Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) is the national standards body of India, responsible for developing and promoting standards for products, processes, and services. For the iPACE-CHIP implantable pacemaker, BIS standards complement the CDSCO regulatory framework and may be required for specific aspects of device compliance. This chapter provides a comprehensive guide to BIS standards applicable to the iPACE-CHIP.

### 1.1 BIS Organization

BIS operates through multiple departments:

| Department | Function |
|-----------|----------|
| Standards Department | Standard development and publication |
| Testing Department | Product testing and certification |
| Certification Department | Product certification schemes |
| Training Department | Standards education and training |

### 1.2 BIS Standards for Medical Devices

BIS has developed standards for medical devices through technical committees:

- **TC 40:** Medical devices and equipment
- **TC 40/SC 1:** Electrical medical equipment
- **TC 40/SC 2:** Implantable medical devices
- **TC 40/SC 3:** Biocompatibility

### 1.3 Standards Categories

| Category | Description | Applicability |
|----------|-------------|---------------|
| IS (Indian Standard) | National standards | Mandatory if cited in regulations |
| ISO adoption | Adopted ISO standards | Harmonized with international standards |
| IEC adoption | Adopted IEC standards | Harmonized with international standards |
| BIS specifications | Technical specifications | Supplementary requirements |

## 3. BIS Certification Process

### 3.1 BIS Product Certification

BIS product certification is required for certain medical devices:

- Mandatory certification for notified devices
- Voluntary certification for non-notified devices
- ISI mark for certified products

### 3.2 Certification Process

#### Step 1: Application
- Application to BIS
- Documentation submission
- Fee payment

#### Step 2: Testing
- Testing at BIS-recognized laboratory
- Test report generation
- Compliance assessment

#### Step 3: Inspection
- Factory inspection
- Quality system assessment
- Process verification

#### Step 4: Certification
- BIS mark issuance
- License conditions
- Validity period

### 3.3 BIS-Recognized Laboratories

Testing must be conducted at BIS-recognized laboratories:

| Laboratory | Location | Specialization |
|-----------|----------|----------------|
| NABL-accredited labs | Various | General testing |
| CDSCO-approved labs | Various | Medical device testing |
| BIS-recognized labs | Various | Specific standards |

### 3.4 Certification Costs

| Fee Type | Amount (INR) |
|----------|-------------|
| Application fee | ₹1,000–₹5,000 |
| Testing fee | Varies by test |
| Inspection fee | ₹5,000–₹10,000 |
| Certification fee | ₹10,000–₹50,000 |
| Annual fee | ₹5,000–₹25,000 |
