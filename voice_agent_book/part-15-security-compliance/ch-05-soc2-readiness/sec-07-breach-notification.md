# Section 07: Breach Notification Procedures

Breach notification procedures ensure the platform meets GDPR Article 33/34 requirements: notify the supervisory authority within 72 hours of becoming aware of a personal data breach, and notify affected data subjects without undue delay if the breach poses high risk to their rights and freedoms.

Breach notification workflow: breach detected (automatic alert or manual report) → initial assessment (severity, data types affected, number of records, risk level) → containment (isolate affected systems, rotate credentials) → investigation (root cause, scope, affected data subjects) → notification preparation (description of breach, nature of data involved, likely consequences, measures taken) → notification to supervisory authority (within 72 hours) → notification to affected data subjects (without undue delay, high-risk breaches) → remediation (fix vulnerability, improve controls, monitor for follow-on attacks).

Notification templates are pre-prepared for different breach types and regulatory authorities. The privacy team maintains up-to-date contacts for all supervisory authorities in jurisdictions where the platform operates. Breach drills are conducted quarterly to ensure the team can meet the 72-hour deadline under real conditions.
