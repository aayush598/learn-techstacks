# Section 08: HIPAA Risk Assessment & Management

The HIPAA Security Rule requires a regular risk assessment to identify potential risks and vulnerabilities to ePHI. The platform conducts an annual enterprise-wide risk assessment plus targeted assessments for significant changes. The risk assessment follows the NIST SP 800-30 methodology adapted for HIPAA.

Risk assessment components: scope definition (all ePHI-creating/receiving/maintaining/transmitting systems), threat identification (natural, human, environmental threats to ePHI), vulnerability identification (technical, physical, administrative gaps), likelihood and impact determination (probability of threat exploiting vulnerability), risk level calculation, and recommended mitigation measures.

Risk management: identified risks are entered into the risk register with priority scores. Mitigation plans include: control implementation, risk owner assignment, target completion date, and residual risk assessment. High-risk items require remediation within 30 days; medium within 90 days; low risks accepted or scheduled. Risk assessment documentation is maintained for 6 years and provided to auditors upon request.
