# Section 03: Business Associate Agreement (BAA)

A Business Associate Agreement (BAA) is a contract between the platform (business associate) and healthcare providers (covered entities) that governs PHI processing. HIPAA requires BAAs for any service that creates, receives, maintains, or transmits PHI. The BAA defines permitted uses, safeguards, breach notification, and liability.

BAA terms: permitted uses (the platform may only use PHI to provide services specified in the agreement), safeguards (BA agrees to implement appropriate administrative, physical, and technical safeguards), subcontractors (BA must ensure subcontractors agree to same restrictions), breach notification (BA must notify covered entity within 60 days of discovering a breach), access to PHI (BA must make PHI available for amendment and accounting of disclosures), and termination (BAA terminates if covered entity detects BA violation).

BAA lifecycle: tenant requests HIPAA-enabled account → signed BAA (e-signature via DocuSign/HelloSign) → BAA stored in tenant document repository → PHI processing flag enabled on tenant → BAA renewal (annually or when terms change). The platform offers a standard BAA with optional addendums for specific state requirements (California, New York, Texas).
