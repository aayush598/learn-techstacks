# Section 06: HIPAA Breach Notification

HIPAA breach notification requires the platform to notify the covered entity (healthcare provider) within 60 days of discovering a breach of unsecured PHI. If the breach affects 500+ individuals, the platform must also notify the HHS Secretary and local media. The breach notification process is integrated with the incident response system.

Breach determination: risk assessment (4-factor test: nature of PHI, unauthorized person, risk of re-identification, mitigation measures) → if breach confirmed → notification preparation. The 4-factor test is documented with evidence supporting the determination that a breach occurred or that there is a low probability of compromise.

Notification timeline: breach discovery → 60 days to notify covered entity (500+ individuals: 60 days to notify HHS and media, must also notify individuals without unreasonable delay). State-specific requirements may have shorter deadlines (e.g., 30 days in some states). The notification system has pre-built templates for different breach scenarios and automated HHS portal submission for large breaches.
