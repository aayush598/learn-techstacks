# Section 08: Post-Disaster Analysis & Improvement

Post-disaster analysis identifies lessons learned and drives continuous improvement of DR capabilities. After any disaster event or DR test, a structured post-mortem is conducted within 72 hours. The analysis covers: what happened, how the response performed, what worked well, what failed, and specific improvements.

Post-mortem structure: timeline (event detection → response initiation → mitigation → recovery → full restoration, with timestamps), response assessment (was the runbook followed? were RTO/RPO met? were communication channels effective?), root cause analysis (5 Whys or fishbone diagram for underlying causes), and action items (specific improvements with owners and deadlines).

Action item categories: technical (automation gaps, monitoring gaps, infrastructure weaknesses), procedural (runbook improvements, training needs, communication plan updates), and organizational (team structure, on-call rotations, vendor management). Action items are tracked in the issue tracker with priority, owner, and due date. Quarterly DR improvement review tracks closure of action items from previous events and tests.
