# Section 02: Business Continuity Plan (BCP)

The Business Continuity Plan ensures essential business functions continue during and after a disaster. While DR focuses on technology recovery, BCP covers the broader organization: alternate work locations, communication channels, manual workarounds, and operational priorities. The BCP is maintained by the business operations team and tested annually.

BCP components: critical business functions (customer support, billing, incident response, engineering operations), alternate procedures (how to operate if the platform is partially unavailable—manual call routing, email-based support, offline billing), communication plan (internal team communication via Slack/Discord, external communication to customers via status page, social media, email), and recovery priorities (restore customer-facing services first, then internal tools).

BCP activation triggers: extended platform outage (>1 hour), data center/region failure, critical third-party provider outage (cloud provider, telephony carrier), or security incident requiring extended investigation. The BCP is documented in a central repository accessible offline. Annual tabletop exercises validate the BCP with cross-functional participation.
