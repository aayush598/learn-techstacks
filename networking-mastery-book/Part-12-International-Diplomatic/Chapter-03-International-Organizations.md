# Chapter 3: International Organizations — Networking Across the Global Institutional Landscape

## 3.1 The Landscape of International Organizations

International organizations (IOs) are the hidden infrastructure of global power. They're where:
- Policies are made that affect millions
- Billions of dollars in funding are allocated
- Global standards are set (tech, health, trade, environment)
- Careers are built across continents
- Networks are formed that last lifetimes

For you — an AI-capable tech professional from India — IOs represent a massive, under-tapped networking opportunity. Most IO professionals have degrees in international relations, public policy, law, or economics. Very few have technical backgrounds. Your AI skills make you a rare asset.

### How to Network with UN Agencies

**1. UN India Office**

The UN in India has a strong presence. Key locations:
- **UN House** (55 Lodi Estate, New Delhi) — multiple agencies
- **UNICEF** (73 Lodi Estate, New Delhi)
- **UNDP** (55 Lodi Estate, New Delhi)
- **WHO India** (New Delhi)
- **FAO India** (New Delhi)
- **UNESCO Delhi** (New Delhi)
- **ILO India** (New Delhi)
- **WFP India** (New Delhi)

**Step 1: Follow and Engage**
- Follow each agency's India office on LinkedIn, Twitter/X, Instagram
- Engage with their posts (thoughtful comments, not just likes)
- Share their content with your network

**Step 2: Attend Events**
UN agencies host events regularly:
- International Days (UN Day Oct 24, World Health Day April 7, etc.)
- India-specific program launches
- Research report releases
- SDG-focused workshops
- Youth engagement programs

**Pro tip:** The event is often less important than the reception/networking time after. Arrive for the formal part, network like crazy during coffee/tea break and post-event.

**Step 3: UN Volunteer Program (UNV)**
The UNV program allows you to work with UN agencies as a volunteer. Two tracks:
1. **Online volunteering** (virtual) — research, translation, content creation
2. **On-site volunteering** — field assignments (harder to get but life-changing)

**Online volunteering strategy:** Create a profile on unv.org. Offer your AI skills for data analysis, research, or content creation. Even 1-2 hours per week builds your UN network.

**Step 4: UN Internships**
UN agencies hire interns in India and abroad. Most are:
- 3-6 months duration
- Often unpaid (financial hurdle — check if any funding available)
- Highly competitive (dozens to hundreds per position)
- **But:** Your AI background makes you stand out

**Application strategy:**
- Customize resume for each agency (don't send generic)
- Highlight "AI for development" angle
- Get recommendation from a UN-connected professor/mentor
- Apply 3-4 months before start date

**Step 5: UN Young Professionals Programme (YPP)**
The YPP is the main entry point for professional careers at the UN Secretariat. Competitive exam-based recruitment for nationals of under-represented countries (India sometimes is, sometimes isn't — check current list).

**Step 6: UN Consultancies**
UN agencies hire external consultants. If you run a company or have expertise, you can get short-term consultancy gigs. AI-related needs include:
- Data analysis
- Machine learning model development
- Digital transformation strategy
- AI governance research
- Tech scouting

**Where to find:**
- UN agency websites (procurement/careers sections)
- LinkedIn (UN-related consultancy posts)
- UN Global Marketplace (un.org/Depts/ptd)

## 3.4 World Economic Forum and Davos

### Understanding the WEF

The World Economic Forum is the ultimate networking organization. It's not just the Davos meeting in January — it's a year-round organization with multiple initiatives and communities.

### WEF Communities You Can Join

**1. Global Shapers Community**
The most accessible WEF community for young people (ages 18-27). 500+ city-based hubs worldwide, including 30+ in India.

**What you get:**
- Local hub membership (meetings, projects, events)
- Regional and global Shaper events
- Access to the Shapers network (20,000+ members across 150+ countries)
- Possible ticket to Davos (highly competitive)
- Projects that create local impact

**How to join:**
1. Find your nearest Global Shapers hub (globalshapers.org)
2. They have an annual application cycle (January-March typically)
3. Application includes: essay, interview, project proposal
4. Acceptance rate: varies by hub (some 10-20%)

**Cost:** Minimal (¥1000-5000/year per hub)

**2. Young Global Leaders (YGL)**
For people under 40 who've achieved significant success. More exclusive (100 selected globally per year). Hard to get unless you're already well-known.

**3. Technology Pioneers**
For early-stage tech companies disrupting industries. If you build an AI startup, you can apply. Winners get Davos access and global visibility.

**4. WEF Expert Network**
As you build your reputation, you can be added to WEF's expert database. Journalists and WEF staff reach out for comments.

### Davos — How to Get In (Realistically)

The big question. Standard delegate pass: ¥8-10 lakh (not happening).

**Alternative Entry Points:**

1. **Become a Global Shaper** — some Shapers get selected for "Shapers at Davos" program
2. **Become a Technology Pioneer** — your startup gets a Davos pass
3. **Be a speaker** — if WEF chooses you as a speaker, they cover costs
4. **Media credential** — if you write for a recognized publication
5. **Partner organization** — WEF partner organizations get passes
6. **Livestream** — most sessions are livestreamed. Watch for free. Network virtually.

**Realistic path for you:** Become a Global Shaper → Get selected for regional Shaper events → Build relationships with WEF staff → Eventually get Davos access (2-5 year timeframe).

### WEF India-Specific Opportunities

- **India Economic Summit** — WEF's India event, held annually/ bi-annually
- **WEF Centre for the Fourth Industrial Revolution (C4IR) India** — located in Mumbai, focuses on tech governance
- **WEF New Champions Meeting** — also called "Summer Davos," held in China

### Fellowship Application Strategy

**These are competitive (1-5% acceptance). But here's the real strategy:**

1. **Apply even if you think you won't get it** — many India applicants self-select out
2. **Use your AI angle** — "AI for global development" is a compelling narrative
3. **Build your profile first** — research papers, open-source projects, volunteering, leadership
4. **Get strong recommendations** — from professors, mentors, and professionals who know your work
5. **Write killer essays** — specific, personal, showing impact and vision
6. **Practice interviews** — most have final round interviews

- Year 1: Build profile (paper, project, community work)
- Year 2: Apply to 2-3 medium-competition programs
- Year 3: Apply to 3-5 top programs
- Aim for at least one acceptance in 2-3 years

**Alternative:** If you don't get the most competitive, find less-known but high-value programs.
