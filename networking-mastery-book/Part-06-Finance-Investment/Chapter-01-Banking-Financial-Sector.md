## Networking with Banking Professionals

### The Tiered Approach

**Tier 1: Branch Managers (Most Accessible)**
Your local SBI/HDFC/ICICI branch manager is the easiest banking professional to meet. And they know everyone in your city.

**How to Connect:**
1. Open an account if you don't have one
2. Visit the branch once a month for minor transactions
3. Build a friendly relationship with the manager
4. Show genuine interest in their work
5. Offer: "I'm working on AI solutions for banking. Would love your perspective on what problems branch banking faces."

**What Branch Managers Can Do for You:**
- Introduce you to the area manager (regional manager)
- Share insights on local business owners
- Tell you about banking events in the city
- Refer you to the bank's technology team

**Tier 2: Regional Managers and Zonal Heads**
These people manage 50-200 branches and report to headquarters.

**How to Connect:**
- Attend bank-sponsored events (financial literacy camps, customer meets)
- Get introduced through your branch manager
- DM on LinkedIn: "Hi [Name], I'm developing AI agents for banking. Your experience in [region/role] would be invaluable."

**Tier 3: Head Office Executives (Mumbai)**
Corporate bankers operate from Mumbai (BKC, Nariman Point, Fort).

**How to Connect:**
- Attend banking conferences (listed below)
- Apply for bank innovation programs
- Connect through alumni networks (IIM/IIFT graduates in banking)
- Submit your AI agent project to bank innovation labs

## Banking Conferences and Events

### Major Banking Conferences

**IBA (Indian Banks' Association) Annual Conference:**
- Held in Mumbai, usually March
- All bank MDs and CEOs attend
- ₹5,000-15,000 for entry
- Strategy: Look for the "Technology in Banking" track

**BANCON (Banking Conference):**
- Organized by IBA and FICCI
- Rotates between Indian cities
- Focus: Future of banking
- Speakers include RBI Governor, bank CEOs, fintech founders

**Global Fintech Fest (GFF):**
- Held in Mumbai, usually August
- 10,000+ attendees
- Banking + fintech convergence
- Best event for meeting both bankers and fintech founders
- Student passes available (₹1,000-2,000)

**Fintech Vayam (by NASSCOM):**
- Focus: Banking technology
- Good for meeting bank technology teams
- Bengaluru venue

**SBI Annual Banking Conclave:**
- Organized by SBI
- Focus on inclusive banking
- Good for meeting SBI leadership

### Event Networking Protocol for Banking

**Before the Event:**
- Get the attendee list (often published on conference app)
- Identify banking professionals you want to meet
- Research their role and recent initiatives

**During the Event:**

## Building Rapport with Branch Managers

### The Step-by-Step Process

**Step 1: Become a Regular Customer**
- Open a savings account if you don't have one
- Visit the branch physically once a month
- Use the same branch consistently (not different branches)

**Step 2: Get to Know the Staff**
- Learn the names of the branch manager, assistant manager, and cashier
- Say hello every time you visit
- Ask about their day (genuinely)

**Step 3: Offer Value from Your Expertise**
- After 3-4 visits, bring up your work:
  "I work with AI agents. I've been thinking about how banks could use AI for [problem]. Does your branch face this challenge?"

- Most branch managers will say "We have some systems, but they don't work well"

**Step 4: Offer a Free Demo**
- Build a small AI agent for a specific banking problem
- Show it to the branch manager
- They'll be impressed and may share it with regional management

**Step 5: Get Introduced Upward**
- The branch manager can introduce you to:
  - Area Manager
  - Regional Technology Head
  - Other branch managers
  - Their HNI customers

### Real Example

A a leading IT company employee (similar to you) in Pune built a simple AI agent that automated responses to common customer queries at his local SBI branch. He showed it to the branch manager, who was impressed. The branch manager introduced him to the regional manager, who connected him with SBI's innovation team in Mumbai. Within 3 months, he was consulting with SBI's central technology team on AI agents.
