### The Major Conferences: Your Networking Goldmines

#### Nullcon, Goa (March/April every year)

Nullcon is India's premier security conference. Held in Goa, it attracts 1500+ security professionals from across India and abroad. Here's how to work it:

**Before Nullcon:**
- Buy your ticket 3 months early (₹3000-5000 for early bird, worth every rupee)
- Look at the speaker list 2 months before. Identify 20 people you must meet.
- Research each person: their GitHub, Twitter/X, LinkedIn, blog, recent talks
- Prepare 3 specific questions for each person based on their work
- Create a custom business card with your LinkedIn, GitHub, and a tagline like "AI Security Researcher | a leading IT company"

**During Nullcon:**
- Day 1 workshops: Skip the popular ones. Pick niche workshops with 15-20 people. Smaller rooms = easier networking.
- Talk breaks: Stand near the coffee counter, not the food counter. Coffee lines are shorter, conversations are longer.
- Evening parties: This is where real connections happen. Nullcon has evening socials. Be there. Have 2 beers maximum — you need clarity.
- The "powder room" technique: The speaker green room area. Don't sneak in, but hang around nearby. Speakers come out for fresh air.
- Business card exchange: Collect cards, write one memory trigger on the back of each (e.g., "Nullcon Goa, discussed AI fuzzing")

**After Nullcon:**
- Connect on LinkedIn within 24 hours. Reference something specific: "Hey [Name], really enjoyed our discussion about [topic] during the [session] at Nullcon."
- Share your learnings on LinkedIn as a thread. Tag 3-5 people you met. This gives them visibility, and they remember you.
- Send a "resources follow-up" email: "You mentioned [tool/book/resource] — here's a link and my notes on it."

#### BSides Delhi (July/August)

BSides Delhi is community-driven, cheaper (₹500-1000), and more intimate. This is your best entry point.

**Why BSides is better for introverts:**
- No corporate booth noise. It's pure community.
- Open stage: You can literally walk up and give a 5-minute lightning talk. No selection committee.
- Smaller attendance (300-500 people). You'll see the same faces repeatedly.
- Workshops are hands-on. You work next to people, which naturally breaks ice.

**Your BSides Delhi strategy:**
1. Volunteer. The BSides organizing team always needs help. Volunteering = access to organizers = access to speakers = access to VIP areas.
2. Submit a lightning talk on day 1 about "Using AI Agents for Automated Vulnerability Scanning." It doesn't need to be perfect. BSides values enthusiasm over polish.
3. Attend the workshop "Breaking into Cybersecurity" or similar. These have 20-30 newbies. You'll find peers AND mentors.

#### c0c0n, Kochi (October)

c0c0n is South India's biggest security conference. Held in Kochi, it has a different crowd — more corporate, more government.

**c0c0n networking strategy:**
- Pre-conference workshop: The day before c0c0n, there are paid workshops. The ₹5000-8000 investment is worth it for the small-group interaction.
- Government track: c0c0n has strong government participation. CERT-In officials, state police cyber cells, NIC representatives. These people don't attend other conferences.
- Dinner networking: The conference dinner is organized and structured. Don't sit with friends. Sit at a table with strangers. "Hi, I'm [Name], I work at a tech company but I'm building AI security tools on the side. What brings you to c0c0n?"

#### GroundZero, Hyderabad (January)

GroundZero is organized by the Hacker Conference community. It's more technical, more hardcore.

**GroundZero approach:**
- This is where the actual vulnerability researchers hang out. People who find CVEs for fun.

### Government Cybersecurity Networking

CERT-In is the national nodal agency for cybersecurity. It operates under MeitY.

**Connecting with CERT-In:**

- CERT-In conducts workshops and training programs. Attend them. You'll meet government officials.
- CERT-In's annual conference: Indian Cyber Threat Scenario and Response. This is where CISOs of PSUs, government departments, and banks gather.

### Ethical Boundaries and Legal Compliance

Indian cybersecurity operates within a specific legal framework:

- **IT Act 2000 (amended 2008)**: Section 66 covers computer-related offenses. Section 70B gives CERT-In special powers.
- **CERT-In directions (April 2022)**: Mandatory breach reporting within 6 hours, VPN providers must store logs, data localization requirements.
- **National Cyber Security Policy 2013**: Framework document for cybersecurity in India.

**What NOT to do (real consequences):**

- Don't run vulnerability scans on systems you don't own. A student from VIT scanned Paytm's systems without authorization — got a legal notice and was banned from the platform.
- Don't disclose vulnerabilities publicly before the company fixes them. Responsible disclosure is critical.
- Don't join Telegram groups that share stolen data or cracked tools. This destroys your career before it starts.
- Don't fake credentials. The security community is small. You will be caught.

**Responsible disclosure process:**
1. Discover vulnerability
2. Report to company's security team (find contact at security@company.com or bug bounty program)
3. Wait 90-120 days for fix
4. Only after fix, write a blog post or disclose publicly
5. If no response after 120 days, contact CERT-In
