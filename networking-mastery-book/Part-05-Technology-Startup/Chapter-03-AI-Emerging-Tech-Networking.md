### The Paper-Reading DM Strategy

When a researcher publishes a new paper:

1. Read the abstract and conclusion (at minimum)
2. Find ONE specific thing you genuinely find interesting
3. Send a DM on Twitter:

"Hi [Name], your paper on [topic] is fascinating. I'm particularly interested in your approach to [specific technique]. I built an AI agent that touches on [related aspect]. Curious if you've considered applying this to [domain]?"

**Why this works:** Researchers love when people actually read and understand their work. Most comments are "Great paper!" Your specific insight shows you're serious.

### ArXiv Daily Habits

1. Subscribe to arxiv-sanity (by Andrej Karpathy) or use arxiv.org
2. Every morning, scan new AI papers
3. Save 1-2 that are relevant to your interests
4. Read the abstract during breakfast
5. Follow the authors on Twitter
6. Engage with their threads about the paper

### AI Research Paper Reading Groups

**Join Existing Groups:**
- ML Research Reading Group (Discord) - active community
- Papers with Code (Slack) - discusses implementations
- Indian ML Research Circle (Telegram) - connects Indian AI researchers

**Start Your Own Group:**

This is a POWERFUL networking move. Here's how:

1. Announce on LinkedIn: "Starting a weekly AI paper reading group. DM if interested."
2. Choose 1 paper per week (start with accessible papers)
3. Use Google Meet/Zoom for weekly calls
4. Invite the paper's author to join your call (they often say yes)
5. Record and post on LinkedIn/YouTube

**Real Example:** Ankit from Pune started an AI paper reading group on LinkedIn with 5 members. Within 3 months, he had 50+ regular attendees including PhD students from IITs. One attendee was a researcher at Microsoft Research India who later offered him a research internship.

### Writing AI Articles That Get Shared

**Format for LinkedIn Articles:**

Title: "[Specific Number] Ways [AI Technique] Is Solving [Indian Problem]"

Example: "5 Ways AI Agents Are Transforming India's Customer Support Industry"

Structure:
1. Hook (Indian context): "India's customer support industry employs 5M+ people..."
2. Problem: "But most support is reactive, slow, and expensive..."
3. Solution with AI: "AI agents can reduce response time by 80%..."

5. Code/call to action: "Built this using LangChain + GPT-4. Code at [GitHub link]"
