### The Cold Outreach Template (That Actually Works)

**LinkedIn Message to a Founder:**

"Hi [Name], I'm an AI agent developer at a tech company who's been following [Your Company]'s journey. Particularly impressed by [specific achievement].

I built [specific AI project] that I think could be relevant to [specific challenge in their industry]. Not pitching anything - just sharing because I think you might find it interesting.

Would you be open to a quick 5-min chat to share my perspective?"

**Why this works:**
- Mentions a leading IT company (gives credibility)
- References specific achievement (shows you've researched)
- Offers value (not asking for anything)
- No jargon or hype (direct and clear)

### The Warm Introduction Strategy for Startups

**Step 1: Build Your Bridge Network**

Before approaching founders, build bridges with:
- Their employees (engineers, PMs, designers)
- Their investors (analysts, associates)
- Their advisors
- Other founders in their network

**Step 2: The Employee Route**

Connect with 3-5 employees of the target company first:
1. Follow them on LinkedIn
2. Engage with their posts
3. Send: "Hi [Name], I'm an AI agent developer who admires [Company]'s culture. Quick question: what's the best way to get my work noticed by [Founder Name]?"

Employees often tell you exactly how to reach their founder.

**Step 3: The Investor Route**

Connect with associates/analysts at VC firms that invested in the company:
- Follow them on LinkedIn
- Attend events they speak at
- Send: "Hi [Name], I'm building an AI agent that addresses [problem]. Given [Company]'s work in [domain], do you think [Founder Name] would find this relevant?"

Analysts are easier to reach and love being connectors.

## Building Relationships Before You Need Them

### The 6-Month Lead Time Rule

Start building relationships with potential supporters at least 6 months before you need anything. Here's the timeline:

**Month 1-2: Follow and Engage**
- Follow 50 founders on Twitter
- Follow 20 investors on LinkedIn
- Engage with 10-15 posts daily
- Comment with genuine insights

**Month 3-4: Provide Value**
- Send 3 DM's per week offering specific value
- Share relevant articles and opportunities
- Offer to make introductions for them
- Build something they might find useful

**Month 5-6: Build Relationship**
- Have had at least 1 meaningful conversation with 10 people
- Know specific things about their business and life
- Have offered value at least 3 times
- Now you can ask for something

### The "Generosity First" Principle

Every interaction before your ask should be GENEROUS. Send them:
- An article relevant to their industry (with your take on it)
- A tool or resource you found useful
- A warm introduction to someone helpful
- Feedback on their product (detailed and constructive)
- A job posting relevant to their portfolio company

After 5 generous interactions, you have the right to ask for ONE thing.

### How to Make the Most of Office Hours

When you get 15 minutes with a founder:

1. **Pre-work (2 hours):** Research their background, read their recent tweets, understand their company's metrics
2. **First 2 minutes:** Thank them, introduce yourself in 30 seconds, ask your BEST question first
3. **Next 10 minutes:** Listen 80%, talk 20%. Ask follow-up questions based on what they say
4. **Last 3 minutes:** Ask for ONE specific next step: "Can I send you my project?" "Who else should I talk to?" "Can I follow up in 2 weeks?"

**The One Question That Works:**
"If you were in my position (a tech company, AI agent skills, no network), what would you do in the next 6 months to build the most valuable connections?"

This question works because founders LOVE giving advice that starts with "If I were you..."

**The "Mention" Strategy:**
Every time you post about AI agents, tag the founders you want to stay visible to. Not in a spammy way - genuinely reference their company or their take.
