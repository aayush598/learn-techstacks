# Advanced Operations

## Intelligence Gathering & Strategic Moves

### Ethical Intelligence Gathering

Information is power. But gathering it must be ethical.

**Legitimate intelligence sources:**
- Public information (news, social media, company filings)
- Conversations (what people tell you directly)
- Mutual connections (what they share with permission)
- Industry reports and analysis
- Conference talks and panels

**What to track:**
- Key people movements (who's joining/leaving what)
- Organizational changes (restructures, new initiatives)
- Funding rounds and deals
- Policy changes and regulations
- Technology shifts

### Board & Advisory Positions

**Pathway to board/advisory roles:**
1. Build deep domain expertise (AI agents + specific industry)
2. Create visible thought leadership
3. Build relationships with founders and CEOs
4. Get invited as informal advisor first
5. Formalize into advisory board

**Your value as board member:**
- AI/technology expertise
- Cross-domain connections
- Strategic perspective

### Philanthropy as Networking

High-value people often gather around causes, not just commerce.

**Strategic philanthropy:**
1. Identify causes aligned with your values AND your network goals
2. Attend charity events (where wealthy/powerful people gather)
3. Offer your expertise pro-bono to foundations
4. Join nonprofit boards

### Strategic Marriage & Family Alliances

At the highest levels, family connections matter.

**Approach:** Not through direct marriage (you can't force this), but through becoming the kind of person that elite families want to associate with.

**Build:**
- Reputation (trustworthy, competent, connected)
- Status (thought leader, ecosystem builder)
- Value (access to AI expertise and network)

When elite families see you as an asset, they'll bring you into their orbit.

### The CEO & C-Suite Playbook

**Getting time with C-suite:**
1. **Warm introduction** (from their trusted network) — 80% success
2. **Direct DM** (compelling, specific, brief) — 5% success
3. **Event approach** (conference, speaking) — 15% success
4. **Content attraction** (they see your content) — variable

**The CEO conversation:**
- Be brief: "I know you're busy, so I'll be direct..."
- Be specific: "I have a specific insight about [their industry] + AI agents"
- Be valuable: "I believe this could [save/make] you [metric]"
- Have a clear ask: "Would you be open to a 15-min call to explore?"

### The Media Playbook

**Getting covered:**
1. Know who covers your space (tech journalists, domain journalists)
2. Build relationships before you need them
3. Provide value (insights, introductions, story ideas)
4. Be available and responsive

**When they reach out:**
- Respond immediately (deadlines are tight)
- Be quotable (have clear, concise soundbites)
- Be helpful (provide data, sources, context)
- Never ask to review the article before publication

### Creating Your Own Events

**Event types by scale:**

| Scale | Type | Frequency | Effort |
|-------|------|-----------|--------|
| Small | Intimate dinner (8-12 people) | Monthly | Low |
| Medium | Workshop/salon (20-50 people) | Quarterly | Medium |
| Large | Conference (100-500 people) | Annual | High |

**The event as networking multiplier:**
- You meet everyone personally
- You're the host (highest status position)
- You control the guest list
- You create the connections
