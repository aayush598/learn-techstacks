# Maintenance System

## Keeping Your Network Alive and Growing

### The Maintenance Challenge

A network without maintenance decays. People forget you. Relationships weaken. You become a stranger to people you once knew well.

**The decay curve:**
- After 30 days without contact: 50% relationship strength loss
- After 90 days: 75% loss
- After 1 year: 95% loss (essentially a stranger)

**Your job:** Create a maintenance system that prevents decay.

### The Relationship Cadence

| Relationship Level | Contact Frequency | Method |
|-------------------|-------------------|--------|
| Strategic Ally | Weekly | Call, meet, or significant DM |
| Strong Connection | Bi-weekly | DM, share, or quick call |
| Active Connection | Monthly | DM, like/comment on content |
| Casual Connection | Quarterly | Check-in, share something |
| Surface Connection | Semi-annually | Like, seasonal greeting |

### The 5-10-20-50 System

Per month:
- **5 strategic allies** — 1 touch each per week
- **10 strong connections** — 1 touch each per 2 weeks
- **20 active connections** — 1 touch each per month
- **50 casual connections** — 1 touch each per quarter

**Total: 85 touches per month = ~3 per day**

### The Maintenance Touch

**Low effort (30 seconds):**
- Like their post
- Quick comment
- "Saw this and thought of you" (share article)

**Medium effort (2-3 minutes):**
- Personalized DM
- Share resource with context
- Congratulate on achievement

**High effort (10-15 minutes):**
- Review their work and give feedback
- Make an introduction for them
- Write a recommendation

### The Dormant Connection Reactivation

**Monthly dormant review:**
- Query CRM: "Last contact > 90 days"
- Select 10 to re-engage

**Re-engagement sequence:**
1. Like their recent content (low effort signal)
2. If they engage back, DM with context:
   "Hi [Name], it's been a while! Hope you're doing well. I was [reason you're thinking of them] and wanted to check in."
3. If no response, try one more time in 30 days with different approach
4. If still no response, move to "cold" status

### The Seasonal Touch Strategy

**Systematic annual touches:**

**January:** New Year check-in + goals
**March:** Spring update + what you're working on
**June:** Mid-year check-in
**September:** Fall reset + ask about their projects
**December:** Year-end gratitude + holiday wishes

**Birthdays:** Automated LinkedIn birthday wishes + personal message

### The Network Health Audit

**Monthly audit:**

```
Portfolio Health:
- Strong relationships: ___ (target: >10% of total)
- Dormant relationships: ___ (target: <20% of total)
- New this month: ___ (target: growth > dormant)

Maintenance Compliance:
- Weekly touches completed: ___/target
- Follow-ups sent within 24h: ___/___
- CRM updated: ___/___

Red Flags:
- Connections unreplied for >30 days: ___
- Important connections going dormant: ___
- Imbalance in value exchange (too much take, not enough give): Yes/No
```

### The Value Maintenance Loop

Every touch should include value (even small):
- "Saw this article and thought of you"
- "Heard about [opportunity] — thought you might be interested"
- "Been thinking about your advice on [topic] — here's what happened"

Never contact someone just to take. Always lead with giving.

### Systems for Scale

**When you have 1,000+ connections:**

1. **Batch maintenance days** — Set aside 1 day per week for systematic outreach
2. **Use CRM reminders** — Schedule follow-ups in your CRM
3. **Group connections by geography** — When traveling, schedule meetups
4. **Use tools** — LinkedIn automation for low-touch maintenance (likes, congratulations)
5. **Delegate** — Eventually have an assistant handle scheduling and reminders

### The Golden Rule of Maintenance

**Never let a connection go dormant if you might need them later.**

It's 10x harder to re-establish a connection than to maintain it.
