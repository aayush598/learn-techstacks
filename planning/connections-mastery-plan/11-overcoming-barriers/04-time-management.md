# Time Management

## Finding Hours for Networking When You Have None

### The Time Problem

You need 2-3 hours/day for networking. But you're busy with work, study, life. Here's how to find the time.

### The Time Audit

**Step 1: Track your current time usage for 3 days.**

| Time | Activity | Could This Be Used Better? |
|------|----------|---------------------------|
| 7-8 AM | | |
| 8-9 AM | | |
| ... | | |

**Step 2: Identify time sinks.**
Common time sinks:
- Social media scrolling (avg 2+ hours/day)
- TV/streaming (avg 1-3 hours/day)
- Aimless web browsing
- Commuting without productive use
- Meals alone (could be networking)
- Waiting time (queues, appointments)

**Step 3: Reclaim time.**

| Time Sink | Hours/Day | Replace With |
|-----------|-----------|-------------|
| Social media scrolling | 1-2 | 30 min networking + 30 min learning |
| TV | 1-2 | 1 hour event + 1 hour reading |
| Unproductive commute | 0.5-1 | Podcasts, audiobooks, outreach |
| Total reclaimed | 2.5-5 | |

### The Micro-Networking Method

You don't need 2-hour blocks. You can network in 5-15 minute increments.

**5 minutes:** Send one personalized connection request
**10 minutes:** Comment on 5 posts from target connections
**15 minutes:** Have a quick DM conversation or send a resource
**20 minutes:** Read a domain cheat sheet before a meeting
**30 minutes:** Attend an online event or write a post

### The Batching System

Group similar tasks to reduce switching cost:

**Monday morning (1 hour — batch outreach):**
- Send 10 connection requests
- Send 5 follow-ups
- Reply to pending messages

**Wednesday lunch (1 hour — batch content):**
- Write 2 LinkedIn posts
- Schedule for the week
- Create 2 graphics

**Friday afternoon (1 hour — batch CRM):**
- Log all week's interactions
- Update contact info
- Plan next week's outreach

### The Calendar Block System

Block time in your calendar and treat it as non-negotiable:

```
MONDAY:
7:00-7:30 AM — Knowledge input (reading)
12:00-12:30 PM — Outreach (5 DMs)
7:00-7:30 PM — Event or community engagement

TUESDAY:
7:00-7:30 AM — Content creation
12:00-12:30 PM — Engagement (10 comments)
8:00-8:30 PM — Call with connection

WEDNESDAY:
7:00-7:30 AM — Knowledge input
12:00-12:30 PM — Follow-ups
7:00-8:00 PM — Event

...and so on.
```

### The Energy-Time Matrix

Match tasks to your energy level:

| Energy Level | Best Tasks |
|-------------|-----------|
| High Energy | Outreach, calls, meetings, content creation |
| Medium Energy | Learning, engagement, CRM updates |
| Low Energy | Reading, listening to podcasts, lightweight engagement |

### Stacking Habits

Attach networking habits to existing habits:

- Morning coffee → Read news (already sitting, add 10 min reading)
- Lunch break → Send 3 DMs (already on phone)
- Evening wind-down → Log CRM (already relaxing)
- Commute → Listen to industry podcast (already traveling)
- Waiting for appointment → Send connection request (already waiting)

### The 1% Rule

Improve by 1% each day:
- Day 1: Send 1 connection request
- Day 2: Send 1 request + 1 comment
- Day 3: Send 1 request + 1 comment + read 1 article
- Day 30: You're doing 30+ actions/day

Small incremental growth builds massive results.

### Saying No to What Doesn't Matter

Time for networking must come from somewhere. You need to say no:

**Say no to:**
- Low-value entertainment (mindless scrolling, bad TV)
- Events that don't align with your goals
- People who drain your energy without giving back
- Perfectionism (good enough action > perfect inaction)
- Multitasking (half-focusing on two things = doing neither well)

### The Minimum Viable Day

On your busiest days:

**5-minute version:**
- Send 1 connection request
- Comment on 1 post
- Done. (This is still progress.)

**15-minute version:**
- Send 3 connection requests
- Comment on 3 posts
- Read 1 article
- Done.

**30-minute version:**
- Send 5 connection requests
- Comment on 5 posts
- Send 2 follow-ups
- Read 1 domain cheat sheet

### Weekly Time Budget (Recommended)

| Activity | Hours/Week | % |
|----------|-----------|---|
| Outreach & engagement | 5 | 30% |
| Content creation | 3 | 18% |
| Learning & knowledge | 3 | 18% |
| Meetings & calls | 3 | 18% |
| Events | 2 | 12% |
| CRM & admin | 1 | 6% |
| **Total** | **17** | **~2.5 hours/day** |

### When You Truly Have No Time

Focus on the single highest-leverage activity:
**Send 3 personalized connection requests per day.**

That's 90 requests/month. At 30% acceptance, that's 27 new connections/month. In one year: 324 new connections. All from 3 minutes/day.
