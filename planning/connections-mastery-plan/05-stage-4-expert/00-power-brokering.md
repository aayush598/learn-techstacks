# Power Brokering: Making Things Happen

## Chapter 25: From Connector to Power Broker

### The Power Broker Identity

A power broker doesn't just connect people — they make things happen. They facilitate deals, resolve conflicts, create opportunities, and shape outcomes.

### The Brokering Framework

**Identify brokering opportunities:**
```
Listen for:
- "I wish I knew someone in [domain]"
- "I need to find [resource/partner]"
- "We're struggling with [problem]"
- "If only I could connect with [person/organization]"

Your response:
"I might be able to help with that..."
```

**Types of brokering:**
1. **Connection brokering** — Who needs to meet whom
2. **Opportunity brokering** — Who needs what opportunity
3. **Resource brokering** — Who has what others need
4. **Knowledge brokering** — Who knows what others need to know
5. **Deal brokering** — Who should do business with whom

### The Brokering Process

**Step 1: Identify need** (from your network conversations)
**Step 2: Match with resource** (someone in your network)
**Step 3: Get permission** (ask both sides before connecting)
**Step 4: Make introduction** (with context and value proposition)
**Step 5: Follow up** (did they connect? Was it valuable?)
**Step 6: Get feedback** (what could be better?)

### Building a Reputation as a Broker

- Be discreet (never share sensitive information)
- Be reliable (follow through on every introduction)
- Be impartial (represent both sides fairly)
- Be value-focused (the goal is mutual benefit)
- Never take sides in conflicts (stay Switzerland)

### The Deal Flow System

Track potential deals and collaborations:

```
Opportunity: [Description]
Parties Involved: [Person A] + [Person B]
Value at Stake: [Value]
Stage: [Identified/Introduced/Negotiating/Closed]
My Role: [Matchmaker/Facilitator/Advisor]
Outcome: [Success/Learning]
```

### Commission Without Commission

Power brokers often work for free (initially). The payoff comes in:
- Access to future deals
- Favors owed
- Reputation growth
- Insider information
- Preferential treatment in your own endeavors

**Eventually:** Advisory shares, success fees, board positions, carried interest.
