# Global Network: International Connections

## Chapter 28: The Worldwide Network

### Why Global Matters

- Cross-border opportunities (arbitrage between countries)
- Geopolitical awareness (understand power shifts)
- Access to global talent and capital
- Diverse perspectives (better decision-making)
- Higher status (global network > local network)

### International Expansion Strategy

**Phase 1: Digital Global (3-6 months)**
- Connect with people in 10+ countries on LinkedIn
- Engage in international online communities
- Participate in global virtual events
- Build relationships with expats and diaspora communities

**Phase 2: Targeted International (6-12 months)**
- Choose 3 priority countries/regions
- Study their business culture, politics, economy
- Build 10+ connections in each
- Plan travels

**Phase 3: Physical International (12-24 months)**
- Travel to 2 countries per year
- Attend international conferences
- Meet local connections in person
- Build local presence

### Priority Countries/Regions

**Based on AI agent opportunity:**
1. USA (Silicon Valley, New York, Boston) — AI capital
2. UK (London) — Finance + AI
3. Singapore — Asian hub for tech
4. UAE (Dubai) — Emerging AI hub
5. Germany (Berlin) — European tech
6. Japan (Tokyo) — Robotics + AI
7. India (Bangalore) — Tech talent
8. Israel (Tel Aviv) — Cybersecurity + AI

### International Networking Protocol

**Cultural considerations:**
- Research business culture before engaging
- Respect time zones (schedule thoughtfully)
- Learn basic greetings in local language
- Understand gift-giving, hierarchy norms
- Be patient (some cultures require more relationship-building before business)

**Virtual international connection:**
```
"Hi [Name], I've been following the AI agent ecosystem in [Country] and I'm impressed by [specific]. I'd love to learn more about the landscape there — would you be open to a brief chat?"
```

**When traveling:**
- Schedule meetings before you arrive
- Stay in central business districts
- Attend local meetups
- Ask locals for recommendations (builds rapport)
