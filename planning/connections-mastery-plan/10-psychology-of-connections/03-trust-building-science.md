# Trust-Building Science

## How Trust is Built, Broken, and Restored

### The Trust Equation Revisited

```
Trust = (Credibility + Reliability + Intimacy) / Self-Orientation
```

Each component can be deliberately developed.

### Credibility

**Definition:** Do people believe what you say?

**Building credibility:**
1. **Demonstrate competence** — Share your knowledge, build impressive projects, get results
2. **Be honest about what you don't know** — "I don't know" is more credible than faking it
3. **Use data and specifics** — Vague claims are suspect; specific claims are credible
4. **Get third-party validation** — Recommendations, endorsements, case studies
5. **Be consistent** — Your words and actions should align

**Credibility destroyers:**
- Exaggerating your experience or results
- Making promises you can't keep
- Speaking with false certainty
- Name-dropping without substance

### Reliability

**Definition:** Do you do what you say you'll do?

**Building reliability:**
1. **Over-communicate** — If you'll be late, let them know. If plans change, update them.
2. **Under-promise, over-deliver** — Say "I'll get back to you in 3 days" → get back in 2
3. **Create systems for follow-through** — CRMs, reminders, calendars
4. **Make small commitments and keep them** — Build trust incrementally
5. **Be on time** — For calls, meetings, deliveries. Always.

**Reliability destroyers:**
- Missing deadlines or appointments
- Forgetting promises
- Not following up
- Being unpredictable

### Intimacy

**Definition:** Do people feel safe being vulnerable with you?

**Building intimacy:**
1. **Share appropriately** — Reveal something about yourself first (models vulnerability)
2. **Keep confidences** — Never share what someone told you privately
3. **Listen without judgment** — Create safe space for their thoughts
4. **Show empathy** — Acknowledge their feelings and perspectives
5. **Be present** — Put away phone, make eye contact, listen actively

**Intimacy destroyers:**
- Gossiping about others (they'll assume you gossip about them)
- Judging or criticizing
- Interrupting or dominating conversation
- Breaking confidentiality

### Self-Orientation

**Definition:** Are you focused on yourself or on them?

**Reducing self-orientation:**
1. **Ask more questions** — Shift focus to them
2. **Listen more than you speak** — 80/20 rule
3. **Don't one-up** — "That reminds me of my experience..." → instead, "Tell me more about that"
4. **Give credit generously** — Highlight their contributions, not yours
5. **Ask "How can I help?"** — Before stating what you need

**High self-orientation signals:**
- Talking about yourself excessively
- Interrupting to share your perspective
- Always steering conversations to your interests
- Asking for favors without offering value

### The Trust Timeline

**Instant trust (not real trust):** Can happen in seconds, but it's shallow

**Surface trust (1-4 weeks):**
- They believe you are who you say you are
- Built through: profile, first interactions, initial reliability

**Functional trust (1-3 months):**
- They trust you to deliver on commitments
- Built through: doing what you say, demonstrating competence

**Personal trust (3-12 months):**
- They trust you with personal information and vulnerability
- Built through: consistency, empathy, confidentiality

**Deep trust (1+ years):**
- They would vouch for you, invest in you, go out on a limb for you
- Built through: years of reliability, shared experiences, mutual support

### Trust Accelerators

**1. Deliberate vulnerability**
Share a real challenge or mistake you made. This signals: "I trust you enough to show imperfection."

**2. Rapid response**
Responding quickly to messages signals: "You're important to me."

**3. Remembering small details**
Asking about their vacation, their child's recital, their project. Shows you listen and care.

**4. Standing up for them**
Defending them when they're not present. People always find out.

**5. Consistent generosity**
Being helpful before you need anything. Repeatedly.

### Trust Repairs

When trust is broken:

1. **Acknowledge immediately** — Don't wait, don't make excuses
2. **Apologize genuinely** — "I was wrong. I'm sorry." (no "but")
3. **Explain (don't excuse)** — Context, not justification
4. **Make it right** — Concrete action to fix the harm
5. **Change behavior** — Show you've learned
6. **Give it time** — Trust takes longer to rebuild than to build

### The Trust Bank Account

Think of trust like a bank account:
- **Deposits:** Reliability, competence, intimacy, generosity
- **Withdrawals:** Mistakes, broken promises, self-focused behavior

**Rule:** Keep your account balance strongly positive. You'll need reserves for when you inevitably make mistakes.

**Action:**
1. List 5 people you want to build deeper trust with
2. For each, identify: What can I do this week to make a trust deposit?
3. Execute those actions
