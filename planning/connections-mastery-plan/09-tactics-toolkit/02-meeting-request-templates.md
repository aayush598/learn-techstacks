# Meeting Request Templates

## Scripts for Every Situation

### The Informational Interview Request

**For someone you admire (cold):**
```
Subject: Question about [their specific work/topic]

Dear [Name],

I've been following your work on [specific project/area] and was particularly impressed by [specific detail].

I'm an AI agent architect building [your project], and I'd love to learn from your experience in [their domain]. Specifically, I'm curious about [specific question].

Would you be open to a 15-minute call? I'm flexible with timing and happy to work around your schedule.

Thank you for your consideration.

Best,
[Your Name]
[One-line bio]
```

### The Collaboration Proposal

**For someone with complementary skills:**
```
Subject: Potential collaboration: [topic]

Hi [Name],

I've been following your work on [their expertise]. I'm building [your project] using AI agents, and I think there could be an interesting collaboration opportunity.

Here's my thinking: [2-3 sentence value proposition]

Would you be open to a brief call to explore this? I have a few specific ideas I'd love to run by you.

Best,
[Your Name]
```

### The Warm Introduction Follow-Through

**After someone introduces you:**
```
Subject: Introduction from [Connector's Name]

Hi [Name],

[Connector] suggested we connect given my work in AI agents and your expertise in [domain].

I'm building [project] and I think there's interesting overlap with [their work]. I'd love to learn more about what you're doing and explore potential synergies.

Would you be open to a 15-minute call next week?

Best,
[Your Name]
```

### The Conference/Event Meeting Request

**For someone attending the same event:**
```
Subject: Meeting at [Event Name]

Hi [Name],

I saw you'll be at [Event Name] next week. I'm a big fan of your work on [topic].

Would you be open to grabbing coffee or a quick chat during the event? I'll be there [days] and happy to work around your schedule.

Best,
[Your Name]
```

### The "Can I Pick Your Brain" Request

**Better version (specific and respectful):**
```
Subject: [Specific topic] advice

Hi [Name],

I'm at a crossroads with [specific challenge] and based on your experience with [relevant experience], I think your perspective would be incredibly valuable.

Would you be open to a 15-minute call? I've prepared specific questions so we can make the most of the time.

Best,
[Your Name]
```

### The Follow-Up Meeting Request

**After an initial conversation:**
```
Subject: Following up on our conversation

Hi [Name],

Really enjoyed our conversation about [topic] at [event/context]. Your insights on [specific point] were particularly valuable.

I'd love to continue the discussion and explore [specific next step]. Would you be open to another call or coffee next week?

Best,
[Your Name]
```

```
I've been building AI agent systems that [relevant application] and I believe there's a direct application to [their industry/organization].

I know your time is valuable. I'm happy to send a one-page brief in advance so we can make any conversation maximally productive.

Would you be open to a brief discussion?

Best,
[Your Name]
[LinkedIn profile link]
```

### The Cold Email That Actually Works

**Structure for any cold email:**
```
1. Signal: Reference something specific about them (10 words)
2. Credibility: Who you are and why you matter (15 words)
3. Value: What you can offer them (20 words)
4. Ask: Specific, low-friction request (10 words)
5. Exit: Easy way to say no (5 words)
```

**Example:**
```
I read your piece on healthcare AI — particularly your point about clinical workflow integration.
I've built AI agents for three hospitals, reducing admin burden by 40%.
I think your team might benefit from seeing how these systems work in practice.
Would you be open to a 15-minute demo?
If not, no worries at all.
```
