# CRM for Connections

## Tools and Systems for Managing Your Network

### Why You Need a CRM

With 100+ connections, human memory fails. You forget:
- Who you've contacted
- What you discussed
- When to follow up
- What value you promised

A CRM is your external brain. It makes you look organized, professional, and thoughtful.

### The Minimum Viable CRM (Google Sheets)

**Columns:**
```
A: Full Name
B: Domain (Politics, Tech, Finance, etc.)
C: Company/Organization
D: Title/Role
E: LinkedIn URL
F: Email
G: Phone
H: Connection Level (1st, 2nd, 3rd)
I: Relationship Strength (1-5)
J: How We Met
K: Date First Contacted
L: Last Contact Date
M: Next Action
N: Next Action Date
O: Value I Gave
P: Value I Received
Q: Notes (key details, interests, family)
R: Mutual Connections
S: Priority (A, B, C)
T: Status (Active, Dormant, Parked)
```

### The Recommended CRM (Notion)

**Database structure in Notion:**

Create a database called "Connections" with:

**Properties:**
- Name (Title)
- Domain (Select: Politics, Tech, Finance, Healthcare, Legal, Military, Academia, Arts, Cybersecurity, Corporate, Foreign, Other)
- Subdomain (Select: varies by domain)
- Organization (Text)
- Title (Text)
- LinkedIn (URL)
- Email (Email)
- Phone (Phone)
- Location (Text)
- Connection Level (Select: 1st - Direct, 2nd - Introduced, 3rd - Cold)
- Relationship Strength (Select: 1-Weak → 5-Strategic)
- Status (Select: New, Contacted, Connected, Meeting Scheduled, Met, Nurturing, Dormant, Parked)
- Date First Contacted (Date)
- Last Contact (Date)
- Next Action Date (Date)
- Next Action (Text)
- Priority (Select: A-High, B-Medium, C-Low)
- Value I've Given (Text - multiline)
- Value They've Given (Text - multiline)
- Notes (Text - multiline)
- Mutual Connections (Relation - link to other Connection entries)
- Tags (Multi-select)

**Views to create:**
1. **All Connections** — Full table view
2. **By Domain** — Grouped by domain
3. **Priority A** — Filter by Priority A
4. **Needs Follow-Up** — Filter: Next Action Date < today
5. **Recently Added** — Filter: Created in last 30 days
6. **Dormant** — Filter: Last Contact > 90 days ago
7. **Today's Actions** — Filter: Next Action Date = today

### CRM Maintenance Routine

**Daily (5 min):**
- Log new connections added
- Update Last Contact for anyone you interacted with
- Check Today's Actions view

**Weekly (15 min):**
- Add new connections from the week
- Update relationship strengths
- Plan next week's actions

**Monthly (30 min):**
- Review all Priority A connections
- Check for dormant connections
- Clean up duplicates and outdated info
- Export backup

### Advanced CRM Tips

**1. Batch logging:**
After a networking event, sit down for 30 minutes and log everyone you met. Do it while details are fresh.

**2. The 5-minute daily habit:**
Each morning, check your CRM for:
- People to follow up with today
- Birthdays this week
- People you promised something to

**3. Grading your connections:**
Grade each connection A-B-C-D:
- A: Strategic — contact monthly minimum
- B: Important — contact quarterly minimum
- C: Peripheral — contact semi-annually
- D: Dormant — re-engage or archive

**4. Notes that matter:**
For each connection, note:
- Their biggest professional challenge
- Their personal interests/hobbies
- Their family context (spouse, kids, if shared)
- Their goals for the next 1-3 years
- Your shared history (how you met, key interactions)

### Privacy and Security

- Never share your CRM with anyone
- Use encryption for sensitive notes
- Respect GDPR/privacy laws if applicable
- Delete connections' personal data if they ask
- Keep political/controversial notes minimal
