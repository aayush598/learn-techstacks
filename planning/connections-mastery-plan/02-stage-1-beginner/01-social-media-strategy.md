# Social Media Strategy: From Lurker to Magnet

## Chapter 12: Systematic Social Media Growth

### Your Social Media Operating System

Social media is your primary inbound lead generation for connections. Done right, high-value people will reach out to YOU.

### Platform Strategy by Priority

**Tier 1 (Primary): LinkedIn**
- Where professionals live
- Best for: Corporate, executives, politicians, investors
- Goal: Authority, credibility, inbound requests

**Tier 2 (Secondary): Twitter/X**
- Where thinkers and builders live
- Best for: Tech founders, researchers, journalists, thought leaders
- Goal: Real-time influence, conversations, trend awareness

**Tier 3 (Supporting): GitHub**
- Where developers prove their work
- Best for: Technical credibility with engineers, CTOs
- Goal: Proof of competence, open source contributions

### LinkedIn Deep Strategy

#### The Growth Algorithm

LinkedIn's algorithm rewards:
- Early engagement (first 60 minutes after posting)
- Meaningful comments (longer comments = higher reach)
- Engagement from your existing network
- Content that keeps people on platform (native video, carousels, documents)
- Consistent posting frequency

#### The 5-Post Types

**1. The Insight Post (2x/week)**
```
Share a specific insight about AI agents or cross-domain connections.
Format: Hook → Insight → Example → Takeaway
Example: "The biggest mistake companies make with AI agents is treating them as chatbots. Here's what actually works:"
```

**2. The Story Post (1x/week)**
```
Share a personal story with a lesson.
Format: Situation → Challenge → Action → Lesson
Example: "I spent 3 months building an AI agent that nobody used. Here's what I learned:"
```

**3. The List Post (1x/week)**
```
Curated list of resources, tools, or ideas.
Format: Numbered list with brief explanations
Example: "5 AI agent tools that are changing industries right now:"
```

**4. The Question Post (1x/2 weeks)**
```
Start a discussion.
Format: Question + your initial take
Example: "Will AI agents replace app interfaces entirely? I think yes because... What's your take?"
```

**5. The Engagement Post (1x/2 weeks)**
```
Polls, "agree/disagree," fill-in-the-blank
Format: Simple interactive format
Example: "Poll: Will AI agents be primarily: A) Consumer tools B) Enterprise platforms C) Both?"
```

#### The Daily LinkedIn Workflow

**Morning (20 minutes):**
1. Post your content (if publishing day)
2. Scroll feed, find 10 posts from target connections
3. Leave meaningful comments on 5 posts (add value, not just "Great post!")

**Afternoon (10 minutes):**
1. Reply to all comments on your posts
2. Send 5 connection requests (personalized)
3. Accept pending connection requests

**Evening (10 minutes):**
1. Check messages, reply
2. Send 3 follow-up messages to recent connections
3. Log activity in CRM

#### Connection Request Strategies

**The 3 Types of Connection Requests:**

**Type 1: Peer/Colleague**
```
"Hi [Name], I really enjoyed your post about [topic]. As someone also working in AI agents, I'd love to connect and follow your work."
```

**Type 2: Target (higher status)**
```
"Hi [Name], I've been following your work in [field]. I'm building [project] and your perspective on [topic] would be incredibly valuable. Would love to connect."
```

**Type 3: Influencer/Leader**
```
"Hi [Name], I'm a big admirer of your work in [area]. I'm an AI agent architect and I think your insights on [specific topic] are spot on. Hope to learn from your continued work."
```

**Daily Target:**
- Send 20-30 connection requests per week (LinkedIn limit)
- Personalize each one (no default message)
- Target: 50% peers, 30% slightly ahead, 20% aspirational

#### Profile Optimization for Inbound

Your profile should make people want to connect with YOU:

**Features that drive inbound requests:**
- Clear, specific headline
- Compelling About section (your story, your mission)
- Featured posts showing your best thinking
- Regular activity (people see you're active and engaged)

**Inbound request triggers:**
- You comment on their post (they check your profile → send request)
- You're in their "People You May Know"
- They read your post (your post shows up, they connect)
- Someone mentions you (tag in comment → they check you out)

### Twitter/X Deep Strategy

#### The Twitter Growth System

**Phase 1: Lurking and Learning (Week 1-2)**
- Follow 100+ relevant people in AI, tech, and target domains
- Read the room (what does the community talk about?)
- Identify who has influence, who's growing, who's engaging

**Phase 2: Engagement (Week 2-4)**
- Reply to 5 posts per day (add value, not noise)
- Quote tweet 1 post per day (with your analysis)
- Like and retweet strategically

**Phase 3: Content Creation (Week 4+)**
- Write 1 thread per week (5-15 tweets)
- Write 2 standalone posts per week
- Engage with everyone who replies to your threads

**Phase 4: Network Effects (Month 2+)**
- People start replying to your posts
- You get on radar of larger accounts
- Inbound DMs start coming

#### Twitter Thread Structure

**The Educational Thread:**
```
Tweet 1: Hook — "Most people don't understand X. Here's a simple breakdown:"

Tweet 2: Context — "Here's the problem X solves..."

Tweet 3-5: The How — "Step 1... Step 2... Step 3..."

Tweet 6-8: Examples — "Here's what this looks like in practice..."

Tweet 9: Resources — "Want to learn more? Here are 3 resources..."

Tweet 10: CTA — "Follow me for more on [topic]."
```

**The Story Thread:**
```
Tweet 1: Hook — "I built an AI agent that [result]. Here's the story:"
Tweet 2: Setup — "I saw problem X..."
Tweet 3-5: Journey — "I tried A, B, C..."
Tweet 6-7: Breakthrough — "Then I discovered..."
Tweet 8: Result — "The outcome was..."
Tweet 9: Lesson — "What I learned..."
Tweet 10: CTA — "What's your experience with this?"
```

#### Twitter DM Strategy

**When to DM on Twitter:**
- They replied to your thread thoughtfully
- You've had a few public interactions
- They tweeted about a problem you can help with
- You have a genuine question about their work

**DM Template (After Public Interaction):**
```
"Really appreciate you engaging with my thread on [topic]. Your point about [specific] was great. I'd love to hear more about your work in [area] if you're open to it."
```

### Cross-Platform Strategy

**Repurpose Content Across Platforms:**

1. Write a LinkedIn post → Transform into a Twitter thread
2. Write a Twitter thread → Expand into a LinkedIn article
3. Build a project → Tweet about it → LinkedIn case study → GitHub README
4. Have an insight → Quick LinkedIn post → Elaborate in blog post

**Cross-platform connection strategy:**
- Connect on LinkedIn → Follow on Twitter → Engage on both
- Follow on Twitter → Find on LinkedIn → Connect with context
- Meet in community → Add on all platforms
