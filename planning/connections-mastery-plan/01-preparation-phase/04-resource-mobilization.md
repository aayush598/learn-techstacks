# Resource Mobilization

## Chapter 10: Budget, Time, and Energy Management

### The Resource Equation

```
Networking Output = (Time × Money × Energy × Skill) / Waste
```

To maximize output, you need to:
1. Maximize time available
2. Maximize money effectiveness (you don't need much)
3. Maximize energy (physical, mental, emotional)
4. Maximize skill (communication, social dynamics)
5. Minimize waste (bad targeting, poor follow-up, wrong channels)

### Financial Resources

**Minimum Monthly Budget: $50-100**

**Essential Spending:**
| Item | Monthly Cost | Why |
|------|-------------|-----|
| LinkedIn Premium | $30 | InMail, profile insights, unlimited search |
| Twitter/X Premium (optional) | $8 | Longer posts, analytics |
| Coffee meetings (2x/month) | $20-40 | In-person connection building |
| Event tickets (1x/month) | $10-30 | Attend local tech events |
| Professional wardrobe | $10-20/month | Build over time |
| Internet/phone | Already have | Ensure reliable |

**If You Have More Budget ($200-500/month):**
- Conference tickets ($50-200)
- Business cards (quality ones) ($20)
- Better equipment (microphone, camera, lighting)
- Online courses (communication, public speaking)
- Coworking space (for networking)
- Travel to events in other cities

**If You Have Zero Budget:**
- Use free tiers of all tools
- Attend free events (many are free)
- Use LinkedIn free tier (still powerful)
- Request informational interviews (costs nothing)
- Create content (costs only time)
- Volunteer at events (free entry + networking)

### Time Resources

**Minimum Daily Investment: 2-3 hours**
**Optimal Daily Investment: 4-6 hours**

**Time Blocking Strategy:**

```
Morning (1-2 hours) — HIGH ENERGY:
- Content creation (write posts, threads)
- Outreach to high-value targets
- Learning (AI deep work)

Afternoon (1-2 hours) — MODERATE ENERGY:
- Engagement (comments, replies)
- Calls and meetings
- Learning (general knowledge)

Evening (1-2 hours) — LOW ENERGY:
- Events (online or in-person)
- Reading
- CRM updates
- Planning next day
```

**Time Efficiency Hacks:**

1. **Batch similar tasks** — Do all outreach in one block, all content creation in another
2. **Use templates** — Don't write every message from scratch
3. **Use voice typing** — Faster than typing for notes and drafts
4. **Fix schedule** — Same time every day for networking (builds habit)
5. **Eliminate distractions** — Phone on DND during deep work
6. **Combine activities** — Listen to podcasts during commute/exercise

**Time Creation (Where to find extra hours):**
- Reduce entertainment (TV, social media scrolling) — save 1-2 hours
- Reduce sleep by 30 minutes (go from 8 to 7.5 if sustainable)
- Wake up 1 hour earlier
- Use waiting time (public transport, queues) for reading
- Say no to low-value activities

### Energy Management

You cannot build a network if you're exhausted. Energy is your most precious resource.

**Physical Energy:**
- Sleep 7-8 hours (non-negotiable)
- Exercise 15-30 min daily (bodyweight, walking)
- Eat balanced meals (avoid sugar crashes)
- Stay hydrated (2-3 liters water daily)
- Limit alcohol (kills next-day productivity)

**Mental Energy:**
- Focus deep work on mornings
- Switch between tasks strategically (creative → analytical → social)
- Take breaks every 90 minutes
- Meditate 5-10 minutes daily
- Single-task (no multitasking during outreach or calls)

**Emotional Energy:**
- Rejection is data, not personal
- Celebrate small wins (every new connection)
- Have a support system (friend, family, mentor)
- Practice gratitude (daily journal)
- Burnout prevention: take 1 day off per week from networking

### Social Energy (For Introverts)

**The Spoon Theory for Networking:**

As an introvert, every social interaction costs you energy. You have a limited number of "spoons" per day.

**Your Daily Spoon Budget:**
- Cold outreach (5 messages) = 2 spoons
- Live conversation (30 min) = 3 spoons
- Event attendance (2 hours) = 5 spoons
- Content creation (1 post) = 2 spoons
- Engagement (comments, replies) = 2 spoons

**Total daily budget: ~15 spoons**

**Strategies:**
1. **Schedule recharging** — After a high-energy networking event, schedule alone time
2. **Build gradually** — Start with 1-2 interactions, increase as you build stamina
3. **Quality over quantity** — One deep conversation > 10 shallow ones
4. **Use asynchronous channels** — Email, DMs (less draining than calls)
5. **Prepare conversation scripts** — Reduces mental effort during interactions

### Resource Scaling Plan

**Phase 1 (Months 1-3): Zero/Low Budget**
- Free tools only
- Free events only
- 2-3 hours/day
- Focus on digital networking (costs nothing)

**Phase 2 (Months 3-8): Small Budget**
- LinkedIn Premium
- 1-2 paid events/month
- 3-4 hours/day
- Start buying coffee for new connections

**Phase 3 (Months 8-18): Growing Budget**
- Conference tickets
- Better equipment
- 4-5 hours/day
- Travel to key events

**Phase 4 (18+ months): Significant Resources**
- Your network starts generating financial opportunities
- Reinvest 10-20% into networking
- Host your own events
- Hire help for low-value tasks

### Emergency Resource Plan

**If You Have No Money AND No Time:**

Focus on ONE channel only: LinkedIn.
- Optimize profile (free)
- Post daily content (free, takes 20 min)
- Send 10 connection requests daily (free, takes 15 min)
- Comment on 20 posts daily (free, takes 20 min)
- Read 30 min daily (free)

This alone can build 100+ connections in 3 months.
