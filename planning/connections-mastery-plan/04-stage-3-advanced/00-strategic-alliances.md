# Strategic Alliances: Building Power Partnerships

## Chapter 21: From Network to Alliance Network

### The Alliance Concept

A strategic alliance is a relationship where both parties actively work together for mutual benefit. It's beyond friendship — it's a partnership with aligned interests.

**Alliance characteristics:**
- Regular communication (weekly/monthly)
- Shared goals and interests
- Active value exchange (not just occasional)
- Mutual advocacy (they promote you, you promote them)
- Trust to share sensitive information
- Willingness to make introductions on your behalf

### The Alliance Sourcing Strategy

**Identify potential allies:**
- People whose skills complement yours (not duplicate)
- People with access to domains you want to enter
- People with similar values and work ethic
- People who are also growing and ambitious
- People who are already connectors

**The 10-10-10 Alliance Portfolio:**
- 10 allies in your core domain (AI/tech)
- 10 allies in target domains (1-2 per domain)
- 10 rising stars (grow together)

### Alliance Development Process

**Phase 1: Foundation (1-3 months)**
- Regular interaction (weekly/biweekly)
- Deliver consistent value
- Build trust through reliability
- Share personal and professional context

**Phase 2: Collaboration (3-6 months)**
- Work on a small project together
- Exchange introductions
- Share opportunities
- Give public support/endorsement

**Phase 3: Alliance (6-12 months)**
- Regular strategy sessions
- Joint ventures or projects
- Mutual advocacy (speak highly of each other)
- Personal friendship layer

### The Mutual Advocacy System

**Systematic promotion of allies:**
- Mention them in your content (tag them)
- Recommend them for opportunities
- Introduce them to your network
- Defend them when they're not present
- Celebrate their wins publicly

### Building Win-Win Partnerships

**For each ally, identify:**
```
What they need: [Gap you can fill]

```
